The philosophy that formed at the time of the Gupta Empire was mainly Hinduism, but other philosophical schools such as Buddhism and Jainism were also influential. The Gupta period is known as the "Golden Age" of Indian culture, and during this time, great advancements were made in fields such as science, mathematics, and astronomy. Hinduism, which is the oldest religion in India, was the dominant religion during this period, and its influence can be seen in the art, literature, and architecture of the time. The Gupta Empire also saw the emergence of several philosophical schools such as Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Samkhya, and Yoga, which were instrumental in shaping the philosophical thought of India.

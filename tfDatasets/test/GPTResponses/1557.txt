The 2002 military operation in the Philippines was focused on combating terrorist groups such as Abu Sayyaf and Jemaah Islamiyah. Following the operation, various humanitarian efforts were implemented.The Philippine government, together with international organizations such as the International Committee of the Red Cross and the United Nations Development Programme, launched humanitarian aid operations to help the affected communities. These efforts included providing medical assistance, food, and shelter to the displaced civilians.The government also implemented rehabilitation and reconstruction programs to support the recovery of affected areas. Programs such as livelihood training, farm assistance, and community development initiatives were also carried out.Furthermore, civil society organizations and non-governmental organizations stepped in to assist in providing aid to affected individuals and communities. Local philanthropists, religious groups, and charitable organizations also contributed to the humanitarian efforts in their respective capacities.Overall, the humanitarian efforts that followed the 2002 military operation in the Philippines were aimed at addressing the social and economic impact of the conflict on affected communities.

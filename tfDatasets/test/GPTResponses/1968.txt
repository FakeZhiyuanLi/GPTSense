There are many buildings that were originally rectangular wood structures, including:1. Log cabins - common in early American settlements and frontier areas.2. Barns - typically made of timber frame and used for agricultural purposes.3. Shaker meetinghouses - a type of religious building constructed by the Shaker community in the 1800s.4. Saltbox houses - a style of colonial architecture that featured a long, pitched roof in the back and a shorter roof in the front.5. Longhouses - traditional structures used by Native American tribes in the Northeast region of North America.6. Pueblos - multilevel dwelling structures made of adobe bricks, commonly found in the southwestern United States.7. Sod houses - homes made of layers of soil and grass commonly found in the Great Plains region of the United States.

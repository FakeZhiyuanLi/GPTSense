Matthew Arnold viewed culture as the pursuit of the best that has been thought and said in the world. He believed that culture enables individuals to refine their minds and improve their social standing. Arnold saw culture as a way to elevate society and enhance individuals' understanding of the world around them. He believed that culture should be shared and promoted by an educated elite, who should work to disseminate knowledge and promote intellectual curiosity among the masses. Arnold emphasized that culture should include the study of literature, history, and art, and that these subjects should be taught in a way that encourages critical thinking and analysis. Overall, Arnold viewed culture as a way to cultivate an educated, enlightened citizenry that can contribute positively to society.

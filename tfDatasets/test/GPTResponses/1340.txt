A handwritten letter, note, or card that has not been typed or printed will most closely resemble an actual person's handwriting. Other factors that can affect the resemblance of handwriting include:1. Writing utensil: The type of pen or pencil used can have an impact on the appearance of handwriting.2. Paper: The texture, color, and size of the paper can also affect the way handwriting looks.3. Writing style: Each person has a unique way of writing that includes factors such as the size and slant of their letters, the way they connect their letters, and their overall style.4. Mood or emotion: Handwriting can also be affected by a person's state of mind, such as when they are stressed or relaxed.Overall, the best way to get a sense of what someone's handwriting looks like is to look at samples of their actual handwritten work.

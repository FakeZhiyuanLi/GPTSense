Literature expands the horizons of its readers by presenting them with new ideas, perspectives, and experiences. It introduces them to diverse cultures and ways of life, helping them broaden their understanding and empathy towards others. Through literature, readers can explore different worlds and historical periods, which can lead to a better understanding of themselves and their place in the world. Moreover, literature can inspire readers to think critically, question assumptions, and challenge their beliefs. Finally, literature can help readers develop their language skills, expand their vocabulary, and improve their communication abilities. All of these factors contribute to literature expanding the horizons of its readers.

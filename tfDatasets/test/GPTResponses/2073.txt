In literary contexts, different characters may represent harmony, order, and reason depending on the specific work. However, some commonly mentioned examples are:- Athena: In Greek mythology, Athena is the goddess of wisdom, war, and crafts. She is often described as a logical and rational deity who values order, justice, and strategic thinking. She is also associated with harmony and peaceful resolutions to conflicts.- Gandalf: In J.R.R. Tolkien's "The Lord of the Rings," Gandalf is a wise and powerful wizard who guides the main characters on their quest to defeat evil. He is portrayed as a symbol of reason, knowledge, and moral authority, often diffusing tense situations with his calm demeanor and wise words.- Atticus Finch: In Harper Lee's "To Kill a Mockingbird," Atticus Finch is a lawyer who defends a black man wrongly accused of rape in a small, racist town. He is a voice of reason and morality, advocating for justice and equality despite the overwhelming prejudice and hostility of his community.- Dumbledore: In J.K. Rowling's "Harry Potter" series, Albus Dumbledore is the headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He is a wise and powerful wizard who advocates for peace, unity, and tolerance among wizards and muggles (non-magical people). He is known for his calm and collected demeanor, as well as his ability to see the bigger picture and make strategic decisions.- Plato's Philosopher Kings: In Plato's "Republic," the philosopher kings are a select group of rulers who embody wisdom, reason, and virtue. They are described as the ideal rulers who can maintain a just and harmonious society by using their knowledge and judiciousness to make fair and rational decisions.

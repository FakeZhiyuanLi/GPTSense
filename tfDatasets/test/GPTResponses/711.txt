Ibn Sina, also known as Avicenna, lived during the 10th and 11th centuries, and was considered one of the greatest physicians and medical scholars of his time. He was highly respected in the Islamic world, as well as in Europe, where his works were widely read and studied during the Middle Ages. Therefore, it can be said that during the 10th and 11th centuries, Ibn Sina was ranked among medical greats such as Hippocrates.

There were three main types of economies that were being developed in Western Europe:1. Capitalist Economy: This economy was characterised by private ownership of the means of production, distribution and exchange of goods and services. This type of economy was developed in countries like Britain, where industrial revolution had already taken place.2. Social Democratic Economy: This economy was developed in countries like Sweden and Norway, where there was a strong emphasis on social welfare and redistribution of wealth. It was a mixed economy with a combination of free market and government control.3. Command Economy: This economy was developed in countries like Soviet Union, where the government controlled all aspects of the economy. The means of production, distribution and exchange of goods and services were controlled by the government.

The major tributaries that flow through the Danube originate from various parts of Europe. The main ones are:1. Inn River - originates in Switzerland and flows through Austria and Germany2. Sava River - originates in Slovenia and flows through Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Serbia3. Drava River - originates in Italy and flows through Austria, Slovenia, Croatia, Hungary, and Serbia4. Tisza River - originates in Ukraine and flows through Hungary, Romania, Serbia, and Ukraine5. Prut River - originates in Ukraine and flows through Moldova and Romania.

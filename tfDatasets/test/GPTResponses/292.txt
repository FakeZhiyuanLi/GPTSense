The structures of the federal government incorporate the following:1. Legislative branch: This is responsible for creating and passing laws, with the power vested in the Congress, which includes the Senate and the House of Representatives.2. Executive branch: This is responsible for enforcing laws, with the power vested in the President, Vice President, and the Cabinet.3. Judicial branch: This is responsible for interpreting laws and making judgments on their constitutionality, with power vested in the Supreme Court and other federal courts.4. Separation of powers: This refers to the division of powers among the three branches of government, with each branch having a distinct role.5. Checks and balances: This refers to the system in which each branch has some power over the other two branches, to ensure that no one branch becomes too powerful.

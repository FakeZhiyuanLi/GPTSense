1) The Ottoman Public Debt Administration (OPDA): This organization was established in 1881 to manage the Ottoman Empire's foreign debt. It was run by a group of European powers who controlled the empire's finances in return for significant interest payments. Despite criticisms of its management and corruption, the OPDA helped stabilize the Ottoman Empire's finances for a period.2) The Ottoman Bank: This was a private bank established in 1856 by the Ottoman government and French and British investors. It played a crucial role in financing the Ottoman Empire's industrialization and modernization efforts in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The Ottoman Bank was a symbol of the Ottoman Empire's efforts to modernize and integrate with the Western world.

The Declaration of Delhi states that human dignity can only be fully realized in conditions of political, economic, social, cultural, and environmental justice and equity, which includes access to:1. Basic needs such as food, shelter, health care, and education2. Safe and healthy living environments3. Adequate income and social protection4. Equal rights and protections5. Opportunities for meaningful employment and entrepreneurship6. Participation in cultural and religious practices7. Access to information and communication technologies8. Peaceful and cooperative relations between nations and peoples.Overall, the Declaration recognizes the interconnectedness of human dignity with various aspects of life, and emphasizes the need for comprehensive and inclusive policies and practices in order to achieve it.

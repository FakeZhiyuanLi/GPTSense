There are various attributes of mercy in Judaism, including but not limited to:1. Rachamim (compassion)2. Chanun (graciousness)3. Chasadim (loving-kindness)4. Selichot (forgiveness)5. Rodef Shalom (pursuer of peace)6. Tzedakah (charity and justice)7. Emunah (faith and trust)8. Shemirat HaLashon (guarding one's speech)9. Anavah (humility)10. Hoda'ah (acknowledgment and gratitude)Different Jewish traditions and sources may emphasize different attributes of mercy, but these are some of the most common ones.

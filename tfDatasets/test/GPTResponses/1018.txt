Charles Evans Hughes was an American statesman, lawyer, and politician who served in various governmental positions including Governor of New York, Associate Justice of the Supreme Court, and Secretary of State. He was also the Republican nominee for President of the United States in 1916, losing to incumbent President Woodrow Wilson. Hughes was known for his eloquence, integrity, and legal expertise, and is widely regarded as one of the most influential Justices to serve on the Supreme Court. He also played a key role in promoting international cooperation and peace, and helped establish the Permanent Court of International Justice, which later became the International Court of Justice.

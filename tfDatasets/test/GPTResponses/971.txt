There is no one event that has killed 10 million people in India. However, several pandemics and famines in Indian history have caused mass deaths. Some of the significant deaths caused by these events are:1. The Bengal Famine of 1770 - Historians estimate that this famine caused the death of around 10 million people in the Bengal region.2. Spanish Flu Pandemic - The Spanish Flu Pandemic of 1918-19 caused around 17-18 million deaths in India.3. The Great Famine of 1876-78 - This famine caused the death of around 6-10 million people in different parts of India.4. Partition Violence - The Partition of India in 1947 resulted in widespread riots, which caused the death of around 1 million people.5. COVID-19 Pandemic - As of September 2021, India has reported over 440,000 COVID-19 related deaths.

The Minoan "Mistress of the Animals" is a divine female figure with power and control over nature and animals in Minoan mythology. There is no specific individual who was considered the "Mistress of the Animals," but it is believed to have been a prominent goddess in the Minoan pantheon. The most commonly associated goddesses with this title are Potnia Theron or "Mistress of the Animals" and Ariadne, the daughter of King Minos of Crete.

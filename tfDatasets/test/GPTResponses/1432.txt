If TB infection causes a Rasmussen's aneurysm, the symptom that may result is frequent coughing up blood. Other symptoms may include shortness of breath, fatigue, chest pain, and fever. In some cases, there may be no symptoms at all. It is important to seek medical attention if any of these symptoms are experienced, as Rasmussen's aneurysm can lead to life-threatening complications such as pulmonary hemorrhage or heart failure.

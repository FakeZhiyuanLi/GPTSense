Resolution 1624 was a United Nations Security Council resolution adopted in 2005 that aimed to protect civilians from the effects of terrorism while also upholding international human rights law. In particular, it sought to prevent extremism, incitement to violence, and the recruitment of individuals for terrorist acts. It emphasized the importance of strengthening international cooperation to counter terrorism and emphasized the need to address the underlying conditions that foster terrorism, including poverty, political and social exclusion, and human rights abuses. The resolution also called for the promotion of tolerance and respect for diversity as key tools in preventing terrorism.

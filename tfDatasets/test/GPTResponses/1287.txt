God (with a capital G) typically refers to the monotheistic deity worshipped in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. In these religions, God is believed to be the one true deity who created and controls all of existence.Gods (with a lowercase g) usually refer to the multiple deities worshipped in polytheistic religions, such as Hinduism or ancient Greek mythology. These gods are specific beings with their own personalities, powers, and responsibilities.In short, God refers to a singular deity, whereas gods refer to multiple deities. Additionally, the beliefs and characteristics associated with these beings can vary greatly depending on cultural and religious traditions.

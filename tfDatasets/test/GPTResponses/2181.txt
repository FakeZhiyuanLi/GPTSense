Many ancient cultures believed that the heavens were filled with gods, goddesses, and other divine beings. In Greek mythology, for example, Mount Olympus was believed to be the home of the gods and goddesses who controlled the natural and human world. In Hinduism, the heavens were believed to be filled with a variety of gods and celestial beings, including Indra, the god of lightning and thunder, and the Apsaras, beautiful female dancers who were believed to reside in the clouds. In other cultures, the heavens were believed to be the dwelling place of ancestors, spirits, and other supernatural entities.

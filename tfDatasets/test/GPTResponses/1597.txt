Melvin Laird was an American politician and strategist who served as the U.S. Secretary of Defense from 1969 to 1973 under President Richard Nixon. He played a key role in shifting the focus of the Vietnam War from a combat mission to a training and support mission, and he also oversaw significant reductions in defense spending. Laird was known for his pragmatic and non-ideological approach to politics and for his commitment to bipartisanship.

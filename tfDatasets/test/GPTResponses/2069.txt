The region to the east of the Aravalli Range is generally characterized by flat and fertile plains that are suitable for agriculture. It includes states like Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal, and parts of Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan and Haryana. The region is drained by numerous rivers such as the Ganges, Yamuna, Chambal, and Son, which provide irrigation for farming. The area also has a humid subtropical climate, with high levels of rainfall and temperatures that range from hot to mild depending on the season.

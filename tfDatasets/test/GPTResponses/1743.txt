There were numerous individuals and organizations involved in the investigation and report about the Bank of Credit and Commerce International (BCCI) scandal. Some of the key players include:1. The United States Senate Foreign Relations Committee, Subcommittee on Terrorism, Narcotics, and International Operations, which conducted the investigation and released the report.2. Senators John Kerry and Hank Brown, who chaired the subcommittee.3. Various law enforcement officials and prosecutors, including Robert Morgenthau, the district attorney of Manhattan, and William von Raab, the commissioner of the U.S. Customs Service.4. Whistleblowers and former employees of BCCI, who provided testimony and evidence about the bank's fraudulent activities.5. Journalists and media organizations, who reported on the scandal and helped to expose the wrongdoing.

During the 20th century, Lithuania was under the political umbrella of several nations. In 1918, after the collapse of the Russian Empire, Lithuania declared its independence and became a sovereign state until 1940 when it was occupied and annexed by the Soviet Union.During World War II, Lithuania was occupied by Nazi Germany, but after the war, it was re-annexed by the Soviet Union as a republic of the USSR. After the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1990, Lithuania declared its independence once again and became a democratic republic.Currently, Lithuania is a member state of the European Union and NATO, with their political umbrella being the EU and NATO.

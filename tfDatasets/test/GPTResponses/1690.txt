Joy Division's unique style was influenced by various types of literature. Their lyrics and themes were heavily influenced by existentialist philosophy, particularly the works of Friedrich Nietzsche, Jean-Paul Sartre, and Albert Camus. They also drew inspiration from dark and dystopian literature, such as J.G. Ballard's novel "Crash" and William Burroughs' "Naked Lunch."The band's frontman, Ian Curtis, was an avid reader and often referenced literary works in his lyrics. For example, their song "Atrocity Exhibition" was named after a novel by Ballard, while "Dead Souls" was inspired by a novel of the same name by Nikolai Gogol.Overall, the literature that influenced Joy Division's unique style was characterized by themes of angst, alienation, and despair, which were reflected in the band's sound and lyrics.

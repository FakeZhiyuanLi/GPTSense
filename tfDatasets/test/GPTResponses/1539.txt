Some of the instruments commonly played in Japanese mandolin orchestras include:1. Mandola: a larger version of the mandolin with a lower pitch.2. Guitar: both six- and twelve-string guitars are used in mandolin orchestras.3. Bass: a variety of bass instruments such as upright bass or electric bass are used to provide a low end to the orchestration.4. Percussion: drums, gongs, and other percussion instruments are used to provide rhythm and accents to the music.5. Keyboard: piano or other keyboard instruments may be used to provide harmonic support to the mandolins.6. Flute: traditional Japanese flutes such as the shakuhachi may be used to add color and texture to the orchestration.7. Other plucked instruments: other plucked instruments such as the shamisen or koto may be used in combination with the mandolins for a unique sound.

The United Nations has taken charge of various measures to combat child labor, including:1. The Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC): This treaty adopted by the UN General Assembly in 1989 sets out the rights of all children to be protected from economic exploitation and hazardous work.2. The International Labor Organization (ILO) Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work: This declaration sets out the fundamental principles and rights at work, including the elimination of child labor.3. The ILO Convention on the Worst Forms of Child Labour: This convention prohibits the worst forms of child labor, including slavery, forced labor, trafficking, and hazardous work.4. The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs): SDG 8 targets decent work and economic growth, including the elimination of child labor by 2025.Through these measures, the United Nations promotes the eradication of child labor and works towards the protection of children's rights.

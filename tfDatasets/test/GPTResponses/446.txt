These terms are often used interchangeably or incorrectly, leading to confusion about the legal status and implications of a treaty.Convention refers to a widely accepted practice or custom among nations. International conventions, such as the Geneva Conventions, are agreements that establish standards of conduct for countries in certain areas, such as human rights or the environment.Protocol is a formal document that supplements or amends a treaty. It is often used to clarify or add provisions to an existing treaty or agreement.Agreement is a broad term that can refer to any kind of understanding or arrangement between parties, whether formal or informal. In international law, an agreement may be a binding legal document that establishes rights and obligations for the parties involved.When discussing treaties, it is important to distinguish between these different types of formalized arrangements in order to properly interpret and understand the legal implications of the treaty in question.

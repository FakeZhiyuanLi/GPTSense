Ottoman classical music uses a variety of rhythmic units, known as usuls. The most common usuls are:- Sofyan: a 6/8 rhythm- Aksak: an uneven rhythm with 2 groups of 2 beats and 1 group of 3 beats- Ciftetelli: a 4/4 rhythm with a slow, swaying feel- Saz Semaisi: a 10/8 rhythm often used in instrumental pieces- Pesrev: a 6/4 or 4/4 rhythm used in dance pieces- Zeybek: a 9/8 or 9/4 rhythm used in folk music and dancesThese usuls can be combined or varied to create complex rhythmic patterns in Ottoman classical music.

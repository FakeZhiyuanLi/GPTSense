Architecture and fine arts reached their height during the reign of Shah Jahan. The Taj Mahal, one of the world's most famous and beautiful buildings, was built during his reign as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. In addition to the Taj Mahal, Shah Jahan also commissioned the Red Fort and Jama Masjid in Delhi, as well as the Shalimar Bagh in Kashmir. He was a patron of the arts and architecture, and his reign is considered a golden age in the history of the Indian subcontinent.

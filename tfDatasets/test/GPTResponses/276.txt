1. Linear Narrative Structure: This type of narrative structure follows a chronological order of events from the beginning to the end of the story. It is the most common type of narrative structure used in literature and is easy to follow.2. Non-linear Narrative Structure: This type of narrative structure does not follow a chronological order of events, but rather moves back and forth in time. It may involve flashbacks or multiple storylines, and can be more complex and challenging to follow than linear structure.

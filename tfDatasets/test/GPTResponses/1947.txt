1. Oud: A pear-shaped stringed instrument with a short neck and frets, played with a plectrum. It is one of the most important instruments in Ottoman classical music.2. Ney: A type of flute made of bamboo or reed with seven fingerholes and a mouthpiece. It has a distinctive sound and is often played to evoke a spiritual or meditative mood.3. Kemençe: A bowed stringed instrument with a long neck and a pear-shaped body. It is similar to a violin or a fiddle and is played while standing. The kemençe is often used in Turkish folk music and can also be found in Ottoman classical music.

The four corps of the Ottoman Army were:1. Nizam-i Cedid Corps (New Order Corps) - established in 1798, it was a modernized and reorganized version of the Ottoman Army.2. Asakir-i Mansure-i Muhammediye Corps (Muhammadan Victorious Soldiers Corps) - established in 1826, it was created to replace the old Janissary corps and was made up of infantry and artillery units.3. Redif (Reserve) Corps - established in 1856, it was made up of part-time soldiers who were called to serve in times of war.4. Mehteran (Ottoman Military Band) Corps - established in the 16th century, it was a unique corps that provided ceremonial music for the Ottoman Army.

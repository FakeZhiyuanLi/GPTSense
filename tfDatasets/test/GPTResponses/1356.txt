Monotheists believe in the existence of one supreme being, who is the creator and ruler of the universe. They believe that this God is all-knowing, all-powerful, and all-good. Monotheists believe in the oneness and unity of God, and reject the worship of multiple gods or idols. They also believe that God has communicated to humanity through prophets and holy scriptures, and that humans have a responsibility to live in accordance with God's will and commandments. Monotheistic religions include Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

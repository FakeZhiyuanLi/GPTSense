There are several international laws that deal with child labour, including:1. The International Labour Organization (ILO) Convention No. 138 on Minimum Age for Admission to Employment and Work, which establishes a minimum age for employment.2. The ILO Convention No. 182 on the Worst Forms of Child Labour, which prohibits the worst forms of child labour, such as slavery, forced labour, and trafficking.3. The United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC), which requires member states to protect children from economic exploitation and harmful work.4. The African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child, which prohibits child labour and mandates member states to provide education and protect children from economic exploitation.5. The European Union Directive 94/33/EC on the Protection of Young People at Work, which protects young workers by setting limits on working hours and hazardous work.6. The United States Tariff Act of 1930, which prohibits the importation of goods manufactured by forced or child labour.7. The Global March Against Child Labour, a coalition of NGOs, trade unions, and other organizations fighting against child labour through advocacy and awareness-raising.

Steven Spielberg has addressed humanistic issues in various films throughout his career. Some notable examples are:- Schindler's List (1993): This film tells the story of Oskar Schindler, a German businessman who saved the lives of more than a thousand Jewish refugees during the Holocaust. The film explores themes of compassion, sacrifice, and the power of human kindness in the face of evil.- Saving Private Ryan (1998): This World War II epic follows a group of American soldiers as they search for a paratrooper whose brothers have all been killed in combat. The film deals with themes of heroism, brotherhood, and the toll of war on soldiers' mental health.- The Color Purple (1985): Based on Alice Walker's novel, this film explores the lives of black women in the Southern United States in the early 20th century. The story touches on issues of racism, sexism, and domestic violence, and celebrates the strength and resilience of the human spirit.- E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982): This classic family film tells the story of a young boy who befriends an alien stranded on Earth. The film explores themes of friendship, acceptance, and the importance of connecting with others who may seem different from ourselves.

Yes, there are exceptions to the Texas Official Language Act. For example, languages other than English may be used in the following circumstances:1. In the delivery of emergency medical or social services.2. When necessary to protect the rights of the accused in criminal proceedings.3. In the provision of educational services to students with limited English proficiency.4. In the promotion of international trade or tourism.5. In the promotion of cultural activities or events.6. In the provision of health care services.7. In the provision of legal or court-related services.8. In the provision of commercial signage or advertising.9. In the provision of customer service in retail or business.10. In the hiring of workers with specific language skills.Overall, the Texas Official Language Act is not intended to prohibit the use of languages other than English in all situations, but rather to promote the use of English as the common language of communication in official government functions.

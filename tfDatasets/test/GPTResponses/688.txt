There were several events that occurred in the European Union in 1996, including:1. The signing of the Amsterdam Treaty on June 27, which aimed to reform the EU's institutions to prepare for future enlargement and give more powers to the European Parliament.2. The launch of the European Space Agency's Ariane 5 rocket on June 4, which exploded 40 seconds after takeoff in French Guiana, causing a loss of $370 million and setting back Europe's space industry.3. The start of negotiations for the EU's enlargement to the east, with visits to Poland, Hungary, and the Czech Republic by EU officials to discuss the possibilities and challenges of their accession.4. The adoption of the EU's first common foreign and security policy (CFSP) declaration, which set out the EU's objectives and principles for its role in international affairs.5. The establishment of the European Monetary Institute (EMI) to prepare for the launch of the euro currency in 1999, with Alexandre Lamfalussy as its first President.

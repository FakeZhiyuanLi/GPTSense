There are many other political groups that exist outside of the two dominant ones. Some examples include:1. Third parties: These are political parties that are not one of the two major parties. Examples include the Green Party, Libertarian Party, and Constitution Party.2. Independent candidates: These are individuals who run for office without the support of a political party.3. Social movements: These are grassroots organizations that mobilize around a particular issue or set of issues, such as Black Lives Matter or the Women's March.4. Interest groups: These are organizations that work to influence policy on behalf of a particular issue or group, such as the National Rifle Association or the Sierra Club.5. Regional parties: These are parties that have a strong presence in a particular region, such as the Bloc Québécois in Canada or the Scottish National Party in the UK.

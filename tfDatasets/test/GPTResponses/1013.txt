The British Indian Army was composed of soldiers from various ethnic, religious, and regional backgrounds in India, including Hindus, Muslims, Sikhs, and other minority groups. The troops were organized into regiments and comprised of infantry, cavalry, and artillery units. There were also support units such as medical corps, engineers, and logistics personnel. During World War I and World War II, the British Indian Army also included soldiers from other parts of the British Empire, such as Africa and the Caribbean.

Cellulose fibers from plant matter can be used to make a variety of products, including:1. Paper products such as paper towels, toilet paper, and printer paper2. Textiles such as cotton and linen3. Biofuels such as ethanol4. Packaging materials such as cardboard and paperboard5. Insulation materials for buildings6. Composite materials such as particleboard and plywood7. Cellulose acetate, a type of plastic used for film, photographic and x-ray films8. Food additives such as thickening agents and stabilizers.9. Animal feed additives such as roughage and fiber sources.

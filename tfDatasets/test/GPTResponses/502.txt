The architectural style of the Chalukyas was a combination of Dravidian and Nagara architectural styles. They also developed their own unique style known as the Chalukya style. Their temples were characterized by intricate carvings, intricate sculptures, and elaborate ornamentation. They used sandstone and granite for their constructions, with intricate brickwork and plastering. Their doorways and pillars were highly decorated, with intricate carvings of gods, goddesses, and other mythical creatures. The Chalukya architecture is considered to be one of the finest examples of medieval Indian architecture.

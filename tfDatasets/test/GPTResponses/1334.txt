Aging of capacitors is a gradual process that occurs due to various factors like temperature, humidity, and voltage stress. Over time, the dielectric material in the capacitor breaks down, leading to reduced capacitance, increased leakage, and ultimately, failure. Heating a capacitor beyond its maximum operating temperature can accelerate the aging process and cause further damage to the capacitor. It is therefore essential to operate capacitors within their specified temperature and voltage ranges to maintain their reliability and longevity.

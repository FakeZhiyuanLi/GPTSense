There are many mythological figures that have been represented as dragons in different cultures. Here are a few examples:1. Tiamat - In Babylonian mythology, Tiamat was a primordial goddess of the ocean who was depicted as a dragon or sea serpent.2. Fafnir - In Norse mythology, Fafnir was a dwarf who transformed into a dragon after obtaining a cursed treasure hoard.3. Shenlong - In Chinese mythology, Shenlong is a spirit/deity who is associated with rain, thunder, and the sky. He is often depicted as a dragon and is one of the Four Symbols of the Chinese constellations.4. Smaug - In J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth mythology, Smaug is a dragon who hoards treasure in the Lonely Mountain and conflicts with the protagonist Bilbo Baggins.5. Quetzalcoatl - In Aztec mythology, Quetzalcoatl is a god associated with wisdom, wind, and snakes/dragons. He is often depicted as a feathered serpent or dragon.

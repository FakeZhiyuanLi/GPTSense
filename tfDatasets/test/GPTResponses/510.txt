The Lisbon Treaty is an international agreement that amended the previous treaties that formed the basis of the European Union. It was signed on December 13, 2007, and came into force on December 1, 2009. The treaty aims to streamline the decision-making process of the EU and improve its institutional framework, as well as strengthen the role of the European Parliament and the rights of European citizens. It also includes provisions on climate change, energy policy, and foreign policy cooperation.

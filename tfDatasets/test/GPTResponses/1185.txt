In non-English speaking countries, the rule of law is referred to by various names depending on the language and cultural context. Here are a few examples:1. Spanish-speaking countries: Estado de derecho (state of law)2. French-speaking countries: État de droit (state of law)3. German-speaking countries: Rechtsstaat (state of law)4. Italian-speaking countries: Stato di diritto (state of law)5. Russian-speaking countries: Государство права (state of law)6. Arabic-speaking countries: حكم القانون (rule of law)In general, these terms all refer to the same basic principle of governance: that the law is supreme and everyone, including government officials, must abide by it.

Zinc forms stable complexes with a variety of donors, including:1. Water (H2O)2. Ammonia (NH3)3. Chloride (Cl-)4. Carbonate (CO32-)5. EDTA (ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid)6. Citrate7. Histidine8. Cysteine9. Glutamine10. PhosphateThese ligands can interact with the zinc ions via coordination bonds, where the donor's lone pair electrons form a bond with the zinc ion. The coordination number and geometry of the zinc complex depend on the nature of the donor and the conditions of the reaction.

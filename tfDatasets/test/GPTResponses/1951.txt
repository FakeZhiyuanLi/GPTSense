Some of the Middle Eastern instruments that can be heard in Ottoman classical music include:1. Oud - a fretless lute2. Ney - a vertical flute3. Kanun - a plucked zither4. Tanbur - a long-necked lute with a pear-shaped body5. Kemenche - a bowed spike fiddle6. Darbuka - a hand-drum7. Zurna - a double-reed woodwind instrument8. Kudum - a small double-headed drum9. Tar - a plucked long-necked lute.

The Chola dynasty became a major power in South India and established a vast empire that encompassed parts of present-day Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. They were known for their military prowess, maritime trade, and patronage of the arts, including architecture, sculpture, and literature. The Cholas also played a significant role in the spread of Hinduism and the development of the Tamil language and culture. Their influence on South India's political, economic, and cultural landscape was significant and lasting.

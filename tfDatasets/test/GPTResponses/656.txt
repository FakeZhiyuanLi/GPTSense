The rock art found in Sudan includes a variety of images depicting animals, humans, and intricate patterns. The majority of the art is found in the Nubian Desert, and is believed to be from the Neolithic and prehistoric times. Some of the most common animals depicted include cattle, giraffes, elephants, and antelopes. The art is generally painted in red, white, and black pigments, and many of the designs include symbolic and abstract figures that are difficult to interpret.

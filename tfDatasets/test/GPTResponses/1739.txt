Cyprus is a member of the European Union since May 1, 2004. It is also a member of the Eurozone and follows the European Union's economic and social policies. As a member of the EU, Cyprus is entitled to free trade, open borders, and access to the European Single Market. The EU provides financial support for Cyprus, and it must adhere to EU laws and regulations. Additionally, Cyprus participates in EU decision-making and contributes to the EU budget.

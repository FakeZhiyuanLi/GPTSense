Federalist Paper No. 39 is an influential essay written by James Madison in 1788. In the paper, Madison discusses the nature of the proposed United States government under the newly proposed Constitution. He argues that it will be a "compound republic", meaning a mix of federal and state powers, and that it will balance the interests and needs of both the majority and minority factions. Madison also asserts that the Constitution is a product of compromise and represents a "happy medium" between state sovereignty and national authority. Overall, the paper provides insight into Madison's views on government design and the principles that would shape the American nation.

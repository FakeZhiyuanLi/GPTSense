Avicenna (Ibn Sina) was known for his contributions to medicine, philosophy, mathematics, and astronomy. He is widely regarded as the most influential philosopher and physician in the Islamic world. Avicenna's most influential work was the "Canon of Medicine," which was a comprehensive medical encyclopedia. He also wrote extensively on metaphysics, logic, and ethics. Additionally, Avicenna's contributions to mathematics and astronomy included the development of the sine function and the production of several treatises on the subject of astronomy.

Martin Hannett was a record producer, primarily known for his work in the post-punk and new wave genres. He was particularly associated with the Manchester music scene in the late 1970s and early 1980s, and is best known for his work with bands such as Joy Division, New Order, Magazine, and The Durutti Column. Hannett's production style was innovative and influential, characterized by his use of electronic effects and his ability to create spacious, atmospheric soundscapes in the studio.

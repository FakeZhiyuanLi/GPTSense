The Vijayanagara Empire promoted the Hindu religion and culture to unify Indian culture. They patronized the building of many Hindu temples, encouraged the study of Sanskrit and supported the promotion of classical Indian music and dance. The empire also supported the development of a common language, Kannada, which helped in the process of unification of the people. Moreover, the Vijayanagara kings provided protection and support to various arts and crafts, which helped in preserving and promoting the Indian cultural heritage.

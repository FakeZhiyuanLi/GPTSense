Yes, 9/11 led to increased FBI oversight. The terrorist attack highlighted gaps and failures in the intelligence and law enforcement communities, prompting a review of national security policies and procedures. The USA PATRIOT Act was enacted in October 2001, which expanded the FBI's surveillance and investigative powers, including allowing for increased use of wiretaps and the collection of intelligence on suspected terrorists. The creation of the Department of Homeland Security also brought additional scrutiny and oversight of the FBI's counterterrorism efforts.

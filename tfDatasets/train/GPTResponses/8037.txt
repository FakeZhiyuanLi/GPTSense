Executive Order 10925, signed by President John F. Kennedy in 1961, imposed regulations on industries to prevent discrimination based on race, color, religion, national origin, and other factors. The affected industries included:1. Government agencies: The executive order required all government agencies to ensure that their hiring policies and employment practices did not discriminate against any person on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, or other factors.2. Contractors and subcontractors with federal contracts: The executive order required these businesses to provide equal employment opportunities to all persons regardless of race, color, religion, or national origin.3. Labor unions: The executive order required labor unions to include individuals of all races, colors, religions, and national origins in their membership and to provide equal employment opportunities to all members.4. Educational institutions: The executive order required educational institutions that receive federal funding to provide equal educational opportunities to all students regardless of race, color, religion, or national origin.Overall, the executive order had a significant impact on industries across the United States by requiring them to take active steps to promote equality and prevent discrimination in their employment practices.

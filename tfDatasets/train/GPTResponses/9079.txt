Motown Records is the label that became Detroit's most famous. It was co-founded by Berry Gordy Jr. in 1959 and became a prominent record company for African American musicians during the 1960s and 1970s, producing hits such as "My Girl" by The Temptations, "Ain't No Mountain High Enough" by Marvin Gaye and Tammi Terrell, and "I Heard It Through the Grapevine" by Gladys Knight & the Pips, among many others. Motown's success played a significant role in shaping the landscape of music and popular culture in America.

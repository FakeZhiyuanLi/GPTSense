Despite the political instability and fragmentation of the Greek world, this period saw the emergence of great philosophers like Epicurus, Zeno of Citium, and Cleanthes, theatrical comedies by Menander and Apollodorus, and significant scientific advances by scholars such as Eratosthenes and Hipparchus. Art and architecture also flourished during this time, with masterpieces such as the Altar of Zeus at Pergamon and the Great Library of Alexandria.The Hellenistic period was thus not a decline in culture, but a transformation and evolution of Greek culture that left lasting influences on the Western world.

There are many species of wildlife that can only survive in specific environments, as each species has unique habitat requirements. Here are some examples:1. Polar bears: These bears are found only in the Arctic region, where they depend on sea ice to hunt and survive.2. Koalas: These marsupials are only found in eastern Australia, where they live in eucalyptus forests.3. Amazon river dolphins: These freshwater dolphins are found only in the Amazon and Orinoco river systems in South America.4. Giant pandas: These bears are found only in the bamboo forests of central China.5. African elephants: These elephants are found in savannas, forests, and deserts of Africa, where they require large areas with access to water and sufficient food.

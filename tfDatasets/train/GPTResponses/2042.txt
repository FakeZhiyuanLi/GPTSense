Non-self-executing treaties typically require a party to:1. Adopt domestic legislation: The party may be required to adopt domestic legislation that implements the provisions of the treaty.2. Create administrative structures: The party may need to create administrative structures to ensure compliance with the treaty obligations.3. Provide resources: The party may need to provide resources such as funding and personnel to implement the treaty obligations.4. Conduct periodic reporting: The party may be required to submit periodic reports to the treaty body or other monitoring mechanisms to demonstrate its compliance with the treaty obligations.5. Cooperate with other parties: The party may need to cooperate with other parties to ensure effective implementation of the treaty.6. Provide access to remedies: The party may need to provide access to remedies for individuals or groups who have been affected by violations of the treaty obligations.

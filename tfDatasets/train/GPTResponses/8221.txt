The curriculum in England and Wales aims to teach students a range of other topics and skills in addition to dieting and general nutritional information. These may include:1. Physical activity and exercise: Students are taught about the benefits of physical activity and exercise for their health and wellbeing. They learn about different types of activities and how to incorporate them into their daily routine.2. Mental health and wellbeing: The curriculum covers topics such as stress management, emotional regulation, and building resilience. Students are taught strategies for maintaining good mental health and how to seek help when needed.3. Sex and relationships education: Students are taught about healthy relationships, consent, and sexual health. This includes information on contraception, sexually transmitted infections, and safe sex practices.4. Financial literacy: Students learn about budgeting, saving, and investing. They are taught about different types of debt, such as credit cards and loans, and the importance of financial planning.5. Citizenship: The curriculum covers topics such as democracy, human rights, and social justice. Students learn about their rights and responsibilities as citizens and are encouraged to engage in civic activities.6. Critical thinking and problem-solving: Students develop skills in critical thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making. They learn how to analyze information, evaluate arguments, and make informed decisions.Overall, the curricula in England and Wales aim to provide students with a well-rounded education that prepares them for life beyond the classroom.

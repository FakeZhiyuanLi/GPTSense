By 1890, Houston was known for its bustling seaport, which made it a major commercial hub for the cotton industry. It was also known for its thriving livestock market and for being a major transportation hub for railroads and steamships. The city's population had grown significantly, and it was home to a number of industries, including oil, gas, and lumber. The area had also become a hub for education, with the establishment of institutions such as Rice University and the University of Houston.

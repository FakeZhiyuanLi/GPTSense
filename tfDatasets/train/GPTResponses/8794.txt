The sounds "yer" are often vocalized as a vowel sound in certain dialects of English, especially in American English. Examples include:- "beer" (pronounced as "bee-yuh")- "fear" (pronounced as "fee-yuh")- "clear" (pronounced as "clee-yuh")In these cases, the "yer" sound is pronounced as a long "e" sound followed by a schwa sound (the "uh" sound).

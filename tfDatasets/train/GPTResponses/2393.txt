Articles 46-53 of the Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties set out the ways in which treaties can be invalidated. Specifically:- Article 46: Provisions of internal law regarding competence to conclude treaties- Article 47: Specific restrictions on authority to express the consent of a State- Article 48: Error- Article 49: Fraud- Article 50: Corruption of a representative of a State- Article 51: Coercion of a representative of a State- Article 52: Coercion of a State by the threat or use of force- Article 53: Jus cogens (peremptory norms of general international law)

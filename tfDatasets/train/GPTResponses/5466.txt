The TRIPS Agreement (Agreement on Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights) accomplished the following:1. Established international standards for the protection and enforcement of intellectual property rights (IPR).2. Created an effective system for IPR dispute settlement through the World Trade Organization (WTO).3. Encouraged innovation and creativity by setting minimum standards for IPR protection, including patent rights, copyrights, and trademarks.4. Balanced the interests of innovation and public policy by requiring WTO member countries to provide for exceptions and limitations to IPR protection, such as for public health or public interest.5. Promoted technology transfer and capacity building in developing countries by allowing for the use of compulsory licensing and other mechanisms to ensure access to affordable medicines and technologies.

There are several factors that contributed to the decline in spreading diseases:1. Improved sanitation: Better sanitation systems and practices, such as clean water supply, proper sewage disposal, and garbage collection, reduce the chance of pathogens spreading.2. Vaccinations: The development and widespread use of vaccines have helped to prevent many infectious diseases, such as measles, polio, and smallpox.3. Antibiotics: The discovery and use of antibiotics have allowed for effective treatment of bacterial infections and prevented the spread of these diseases.4. Health education: Increased awareness and education about hygiene and disease prevention have helped individuals to take preventative measures and reduce the spread of diseases.5. Public health initiatives: Governments and public health agencies have implemented various programs and policies, such as disease surveillance, early detection, and quarantine measures, to reduce the spread of diseases.

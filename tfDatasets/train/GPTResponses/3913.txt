There are many churches and church structures that have become historical monuments, both in Western and Eastern Christian traditions. Here are some examples:- The Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris, France, which has been a symbol of French history and culture for centuries, was declared a historical monument in 1862.- The Hagia Sophia in Istanbul, Turkey, was built as a church in 537 AD and became a mosque in 1453. It was declared a historical monument by the Turkish government in 1934.- The Basilica di Santa Maria del Fiore in Florence, Italy, is a notable example of Italian Gothic architecture and was declared a historical monument in 1860.- The Wailing Wall in Jerusalem, Israel, is a remnant of the Second Temple that was destroyed by the Romans in 70 AD. It has become a significant historical and religious site for Jews and was declared a historical monument by Israel in 1967.- The San Xavier del Bac Mission in Tucson, Arizona, USA, is a Spanish colonial church built in the 18th century. It was declared a historical monument in 1960.

Early humans (Homo habilis and Homo erectus) used a variety of tools for survival and hunting. Stone tools such as handaxes, cleavers, and choppers were used for chopping and cutting. They also used stone flakes for cutting meat, scraping hides, and other general purposes.Bone tools were also used for digging, hunting, and scraping. Wooden tools such as spears and digging sticks were used for hunting and gathering food. Animal hides were used as clothing and to make shelter.Early humans also developed fire-making tools such as fire drills and flint and iron pyrite used for striking sparks. These tools allowed them to cook food, stay warm, and provide light during the night.

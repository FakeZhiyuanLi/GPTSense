Oklahoma's western climate type is typically classified as semi-arid or steppe climate, characterized by hot summers and dry winters with occasional snowfall. The region experiences low humidity and high winds, with a propensity for drought conditions. Average temperatures during summer months range from mid to high 90s, while winter temperatures generally reach high 30s to low 40s. Thunderstorms, tornadoes, and dust storms are common weather events in Oklahoma's western region.

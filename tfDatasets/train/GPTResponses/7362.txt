There were several factors that often caused strife between Greek states including:1. Territorial disputes: Greek city-states frequently fought over land boundaries and resources.2. Economic competition: The rivalry over trade routes, ports, and valuable resources caused many disagreements between Greek city-states.3. Political differences: Greek states often had conflicting political ideologies such as democracy, oligarchy, and monarchy, and this led to disagreements.4. Cultural and religious differences: Greek city-states often had different religious beliefs and cultures, and this sometimes led to clashes.5. Personal ambitions of rulers: Many Greek rulers had personal ambitions to expand their power and territory, and this often led to wars and conflicts.

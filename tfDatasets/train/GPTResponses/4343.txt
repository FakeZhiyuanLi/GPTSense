No, the effects of serotonin are not limited to the limbic system. Serotonin is a neurotransmitter that is found throughout the central nervous system and peripheral nervous system. It plays a role in various physiological processes, including mood regulation, appetite, sleep, and pain perception. Serotonin is also involved in the regulation of cardiovascular function, gastrointestinal motility, and immune function. Therefore, the effects of serotonin are not limited to the limbic system, but rather, it has widespread effects on various physiological processes in the body.

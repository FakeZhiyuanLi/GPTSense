The two main sects of Buddhism in Japan are:1. Mahayana Buddhism: This sect emphasizes the importance of compassion and the belief that all beings can attain enlightenment. Some of the key sub-sects of Mahayana Buddhism in Japan include Pure Land Buddhism and Nichiren Buddhism.2. Theravada Buddhism: This sect emphasizes the importance of the individual's journey towards enlightenment and encourages the use of meditation as a means of achieving this goal. However, this sect is not as prominent in Japan as Mahayana Buddhism.

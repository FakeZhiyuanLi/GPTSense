Early Asian immigrants to the United States experienced various forms of discrimination, including:1. Racial discrimination: They were subjected to racial prejudice and discrimination based on their physical appearance and perceived cultural differences.2. Economic discrimination: They were often paid lower wages than their white counterparts for the same work, faced job discrimination, and were excluded from many professions.3. Legal discrimination: They were subjected to discriminatory laws, such as the Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882, which banned Chinese immigration and limited their rights as citizens.4. Social discrimination: They faced social ostracism, segregation, and violence, including mob violence, lynching, and forced deportations.5. Cultural discrimination: They were often portrayed as inferior and exoticized in popular culture and media, contributing to negative stereotypes and misconceptions about them.

1. Nusach Sefard - a blend of Ashkenazic and Sephardic liturgical traditions, developed in the late 19th century. It is commonly used by Hasidic groups and some non-Hasidic Orthodox communities.2. Nusach Ari - developed by Rabbi Isaac Luria (1534-1572) and his disciples in Safed, Israel. It is commonly used by Hasidic groups, especially those of the Chabad-Lubavitch movement. Nusach Ari is known for its deep kabbalistic symbolism and emphasis on meditation during prayer.

A referee can choose to end the match in the following cases:1. Serious injury: If a player is seriously injured and cannot continue playing, the referee can stop the match.2. Player misconduct: If a player commits serious foul play or violent conduct, the referee can send off the player and end the match.3. Crowd disturbance: If there is a major crowd disturbance that poses a threat to player safety, the referee can end the match.4. Extreme weather conditions: If there is extreme weather such as lightning or heavy snow, the referee can stop the match for safety reasons.5. Technical issues: If there are technical issues with the playing surface such as flooded or frozen pitches, the referee can end the match.6. Unforeseen circumstances: If there are any unforeseen circumstances that make it impossible for the match to continue, the referee can end the match.

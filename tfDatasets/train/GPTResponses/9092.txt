There are five grammatical moods of Czech verbs:1. Indicative mood - used to make statements, ask questions, or express opinions.Example: Jsem student (I am a student)2. Imperative mood - used to give commands, orders, or suggestions.Example: Jdi do školy! (Go to school!)3. Conditional mood - used to express hypothetical situations or actions that depend on certain conditions.Example: Kdybych měl peníze, koupil bych si nové auto. (If I had money, I would buy a new car.)4. Subjunctive mood - used to express doubt, uncertainty, or subjective feelings.Example: Kdybych byla bohatá, cestovala bych po celém světě. (If I were rich, I would travel around the world.)5. Infinitive - used to express an action or state without indicating who is doing it or when it is happening.Example: Chtěl bych jíst čokoládu. (I would like to eat chocolate.)

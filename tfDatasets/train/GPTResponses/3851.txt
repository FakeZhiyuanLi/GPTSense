An ethologist engages in:1. Fieldwork: observing animals in their natural habitat to study their behaviors and interactions with their environment.2. Data analysis: collecting and analyzing data from experiments or observations to draw conclusions about animal behavior.3. Writing: presenting research findings in scientific journals, books, or conferences.4. Teaching: mentoring and teaching students about animal behavior, ecology, and related subjects.5. Collaborating: working with other scientists from different disciplines to study animal behavior in a holistic manner.6. Grant writing: writing grant proposals to secure funding for their research projects.7. Public outreach: participating in public educational programs to raise awareness about animal behavior and conservation.

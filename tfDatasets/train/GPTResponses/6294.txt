The earliest Christian hymns may have been ancient Jewish psalms that were adapted for Christian use. Some of the earliest hymns that are specifically Christian in origin include:1. "Phos Hilaron" (Light Joyous) - This Greek hymn dates back to the 3rd century and is one of the earliest-known Christian hymns. It was sung during evening prayer services and is still used in the Eastern Orthodox Church today.2. "Te Deum" - This Latin hymn is believed to have been written in the 4th century and is still used in the Roman Catholic Church and Anglican Church as a hymn of praise and thanksgiving.3. "Gloria in Excelsis Deo" - This Latin hymn is believed to have been written in the 4th century and is still used in many Christian denominations, especially during Christmas.4. "O Splendor of God's Glory Bright" - This Latin hymn dates back to the 4th century and is still used in the Anglican Church and other Protestant denominations.5. "Veni Creator Spiritus" - This Latin hymn is believed to have been written in the 9th century and is still used in the Roman Catholic Church as a hymn to the Holy Spirit.

There are two treaties between Japan and Korea that were declared null and void:1. Treaty of 1905: This treaty was signed between Japan and Korea after Japan's victory in the Russo-Japanese War. Under this treaty, Korea was forced to become a protectorate of Japan, losing its sovereignty, and granting Japan extensive control over its foreign affairs. The treaty was declared null and void in 1965 by the Treaty on Basic Relations between Japan and the Republic of Korea.2. Annexation Treaty of 1910: This treaty was signed between Japan and Korea, under which Korea was annexed by Japan, and became a part of the Japanese Empire. The treaty was declared null and void in 1965 by the Treaty on Basic Relations between Japan and the Republic of Korea.

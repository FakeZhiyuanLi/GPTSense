Autoimmune disorders: In which the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues, leading to chronic inflammation and tissue damage, such as lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, and multiple sclerosis.2. Allergic disorders: In which the immune system overreacts to harmless substances, such as pollen, dust mites, or certain foods, leading to a range of symptoms, including itching, swelling, hives, and anaphylaxis.3. Immunodeficiency disorders: In which the immune system is weakened or unable to function properly, resulting in an increased susceptibility to infections, such as HIV/AIDS, inherited genetic disorders like Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID), and immunosuppressive therapy used to prevent transplant rejection.

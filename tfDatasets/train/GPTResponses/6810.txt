University towns had grown in wealth and importance during the Renaissance era, which occurred from the 14th to the 17th century in Europe. This period saw a resurgence of scholarly and artistic pursuits, and universities played a pivotal role in the history and culture of the time. Cities such as Bologna, Oxford, and Paris became centers of learning and innovation, attracting students and scholars from all over the world. The wealth and prestige of these towns grew in tandem with the growth of their universities, and they became important centers of trade, commerce, and culture.

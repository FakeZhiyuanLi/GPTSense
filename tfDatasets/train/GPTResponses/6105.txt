Quails can be found in various locations around the world, including grasslands, forests, deserts, and agricultural fields. They are most commonly found in North and South America, Europe, Asia, and Africa. In North America, the most common species of quail include the northern bobwhite, California quail, and Gambel's quail. In Europe, the most common species is the common quail. In Asia, the Japanese quail is widely distributed, and in Africa, the helmeted guinea fowl is a common species.

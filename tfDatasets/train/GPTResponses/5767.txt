The nine ethnic groups in Eritrea each have their own styles of music and accompanying dance. These groups include the Tigrinya, Tigre, Saho, Bilen, Rashaida, Afar, Kunama, Nara and Hidareb. Each group has unique music and dance styles that reflect their cultural traditions and history. For example, the Tigrinya people are known for their traditional cultural dance called “Tigrinya Tewahdo” while the Bilen people have a traditional dance called “Bilen Arak” that involves intricate footwork and spinning.  Overall, music and dance are important aspects of Eritrean culture and are often used to express joy, celebration and unity within communities.

Some scholars believe that there may be a connection or influence between the ancient Egyptian goddess Isis and the Virgin Mary in Christian tradition. This theory suggests that early Christians may have incorporated certain aspects of Isis worship into their own beliefs and practices, including the veneration of motherhood and the association with female symbols like the moon and stars. However, this is a controversial theory and there is limited evidence to support it.

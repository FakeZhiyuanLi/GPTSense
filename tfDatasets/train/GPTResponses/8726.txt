Human beings or individuals are typically complicit in 419 scams. In these scams, fraudsters trick individuals into giving them money or personal information through email, phone, or other forms of communication. These fraudsters may work alone or as part of a larger criminal organization. However, companies, organizations, or other entities may unwittingly facilitate these scams by failing to properly monitor or regulate their communication channels or by not educating their employees about how to avoid falling victim to these types of scams.

One distinctive design element of Gothic cathedrals of the Iberian Peninsula is the use of intricate stone tracery in their windows, known as "reticulated windows." These windows often feature elaborate and symmetrical designs with pointed arches and intricate geometrical shapes. Another characteristic of Iberian Gothic cathedrals is the use of highly decorated portals or entrances, often featuring intricate detailing, sculptures, and reliefs with biblical and religious scenes. Additionally, many Iberian Gothic cathedrals have tall spires or towers, which add to their vertical emphasis and grandeur.

The Rock 'n' Wrestling Connection was a marketing strategy by the World Wrestling Federation (now WWE) in the mid-1980s that aimed to merge the worlds of professional wrestling and pop music. The idea was to bring in mainstream music fans and younger viewers who were attracted to the colorful personalities of both movements.The strategy was successful in bringing professional wrestling into mainstream pop culture and attracting a whole new generation of fans to the industry. The Rock 'n' Wrestling Connection was also instrumental in launching the careers of wrestlers like Hulk Hogan, who became a household name, and musical celebrities like Cyndi Lauper, who worked with the WWF in numerous events and their annual WrestleMania shows.

The era of the Scientific Revolution, from the 16th to the 18th century, was an important period for scientific advancements. It was marked by the development of new ideas and innovations across various scientific fields including astronomy, physics, mathematics, and biology. The Scientific Revolution was characterized by the shift from traditional beliefs and superstitions to empirical and rational thinking, which paved the way for modern science. Some of the notable scientists during this era include Galileo Galilei, Isaac Newton, Nicolaus Copernicus, and Johannes Kepler.

1. Acceptance: Other parties may accept the reservation and allow the reserving state to limit its obligations under the treaty.2. Objection: Other parties may object to the reservation if it affects a fundamental provision of the treaty or is incompatible with the object and purpose of the treaty. The objection results in the reservation not being valid for that specific country.3. Conditional Acceptance: Other parties may accept the reservation, but only under certain conditions.4. Withdrawal: Other parties may choose to withdraw from the treaty if they are not satisfied with the reservation.5. Request for Clarification: Other parties may request clarification on the effect of the reservation and the limitations it imposes.

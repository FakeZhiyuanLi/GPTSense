The Aksumite rulers facilitated trade by creating a strong central government that oversaw the trading activities of the region. They controlled the key trade routes that linked East Africa with the Mediterranean world, Red Sea, and Arab regions. The rulers also invested in the construction of roads, and ports to make transportation of goods and products easy. They also established a currency system that facilitated the exchange of goods and services. Additionally, the Aksumite rulers promoted a regular exchange of goods, resources, and cultural practices between different regions, which further facilitated trade. They also managed to maintain political stability and security, which encouraged traders from different parts of the world to participate in the lucrative trade in Aksum.

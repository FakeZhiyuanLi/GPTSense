The beginning of modern science is generally considered to be the Scientific Revolution, which began in Europe in the 16th century and continued through the 17th century. This era saw significant advancements in the fields of astronomy, physics, chemistry, biology, and mathematics, as scholars began using the scientific method to make systematic observations and conduct experiments to test hypotheses.

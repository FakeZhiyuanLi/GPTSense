Civitas Dei is a Latin phrase that translates to "City of God." It refers to the concept of a perfect utopian society or a heavenly kingdom. The phrase has been used in many religious and philosophical treatises throughout history. In Christian theology, Civitas Dei refers to the ideal society built on the foundation of faith in God and the principles of righteousness and justice. It is associated with the early Christian theologian, St. Augustine of Hippo, who wrote a book titled "The City of God" in which he explores the concept of Civitas Dei.

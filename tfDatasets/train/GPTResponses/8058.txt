They are both considered important and influential medical works in Islamic civilization. The Kitab al-Shifa was written by the Muslim philosopher and physician Avicenna in the 11th century, while the Canon of Medicine was written by the Persian physician Ibn Sina (also known as Avicenna) in the 10th century. Both of these works were widely read and studied in the Islamic world and played a significant role in the development of medicine and healthcare practices in that region.

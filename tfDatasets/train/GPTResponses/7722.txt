Religion, specifically Buddhism, has a strong influence over all aspects of Tibetans' lives. It shapes their cultural practices, moral values, social interactions, and worldview. Buddhism, as a major religion in Tibet, permeates all aspects of Tibetan society and is deeply ingrained in their daily lives. It is not just a spiritual practice but also a way of life that guides their decisions and actions. From birth to death, religion plays a vital role in the lives of Tibetans, and they firmly believe in the power of prayer, meditation, and compassion to bring peace and harmony to their lives.

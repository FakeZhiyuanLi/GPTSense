Gorbachev sought support from a wide range of people in order to increase liberties, including fellow leaders in the Soviet government, members of the Communist Party, intellectuals, and activists. He also reached out to Western leaders and sought to improve relations with the rest of the world, recognizing that international pressure would play a role in pushing the Soviet Union towards greater openness and reform. Ultimately, Gorbachev's efforts to seek support from a diverse array of individuals and groups helped to pave the way for significant reforms in the Soviet Union.

Some of the major slave traders in the 19th century were:1. Europeans, particularly the Portuguese, Dutch, Spanish, and British, who had established slave trading networks across Africa, the Americas, and the Caribbean.2. American slave traders, who were involved in the domestic slave trade within the United States, particularly in the South, where the demand for slave labor was high for cotton and tobacco plantations.3. Arab slave traders in East Africa and the Middle East, who traded in slaves from East Africa, particularly from present-day Kenya, Tanzania, and Ethiopia.4. Brazilian and Cuban slave traders, who were heavily involved in the transatlantic slave trade and the domestic slave trade within South America.5. West African slave traders, who were active in the transatlantic slave trade, particularly in the sale of captives to European and American slave traders.

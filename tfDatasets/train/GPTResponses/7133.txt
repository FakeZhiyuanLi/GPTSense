The two fortifications that distinguished the second Fort Hale built in New Haven during the Civil War were:1. The addition of a brick powder magazine: The first Fort Hale was made entirely of earthworks without any permanent structures. However, during the construction of the second Fort Hale, a brick powder magazine was added to store gunpowder and other critical supplies safely.2. Artillery emplacements: The second Fort Hale was designed to support artillery, and several gun emplacements were constructed to accommodate different types of artillery. The emplacements were strategically placed to provide maximum coverage of the surrounding area and defend against potential enemy attacks.

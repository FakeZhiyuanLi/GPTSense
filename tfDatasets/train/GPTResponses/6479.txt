The members of the Order of the British Empire are appointed for their distinguished service or contribution to the arts, sciences, charity, public service, or welfare. Some of the acts that the members may have performed include:1. Outstanding contribution to their profession or industry2. Charitable work for the betterment of society3. Political or public service above and beyond the call of duty4. Sporting achievements at national or international level5. Scientific research that has made a significant impact on society6. Cultural contributions, particularly in the fields of music, art or literature7. Exceptional acts of bravery or heroism8. Improvement to public welfare, such as in healthcare or education9. Community service that benefits the local area or wider society10. Facilitating or promoting international relations and understanding.

There are several pieces of evidence suggesting that wine was made in ancient Egypt, including:1) Hieroglyphic inscriptions found on tomb walls and papyri dating back to 4000 BC depicting the process of winemaking.2) Archaeological findings of wine jars, presses, and grape seeds in ancient Egyptian tombs, including those of Tutankhamun and Ramses II.3) References to wine in ancient Egyptian literature, including the Book of the Dead and the Ebers Papyrus, which is one of the oldest medical texts and mentions the use of wine as a medicine.4) Accounts by Greek and Roman historians who wrote about the wine trade between Egypt and other Mediterranean countries.All of these sources collectively provide evidence to support the theory that wine was made in ancient Egypt.

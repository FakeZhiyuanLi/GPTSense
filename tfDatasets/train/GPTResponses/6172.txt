Seattle celebrated its economic rise in 1962 by hosting the World's Fair, also known as the Century 21 Exposition. The fair ran from April 21 to October 21, 1962, and it was the first World's Fair held in the United States since World War II. The theme of the fair was "Living in the Space Age," and it showcased the city's technological advancements, including the Space Needle, the monorail, and the Pacific Science Center. The event attracted over 10 million visitors and put Seattle on the map as a major player in the American economy.

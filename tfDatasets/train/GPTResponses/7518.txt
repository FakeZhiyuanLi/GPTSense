The earliest records of humans using pesticides can be traced back to around 2500 BC in ancient Sumeria, where sulfur was used to control insects and mites. Egyptians also used various natural substances like arsenic sulfide, mercury, and sulfur to control pests in agriculture and animal husbandry around 2500 years ago. Ancient Chinese and Indian civilizations also used botanical products like neem, sesame oil, and garlic to repel insects and pests from crops.

In most cases, shared meals and potluck events are organized by a group of individuals or a community who share similar interests or values. Examples include:1. Religious groups such as churches, synagogues, and mosques who organize potluck meals after religious services.2. Workplaces where employees organize shared meals as a way of team building and socializing.3. School and university students who organize potluck lunches as a way of sharing their cultural food and diversity.4. Community groups such as neighborhood associations, parent-teacher associations, and local charities who organize shared meals to build a sense of community and social cohesion.

During the Classical era, many aspects of music became more standardized, including:1. Musical forms: Sonata form, theme and variations, minuet and trio, and rondo became standardized forms for instrumental music.2. Instrumentation: The classical orchestra and ensemble became more standardized, with four sections – strings, woodwinds, brass, and percussion – and particular instruments assigned to each section.3. Harmony: The use of functional harmony, including the dominant-tonic relationship, became more standardized.4. Dynamics and articulation: The use of specific dynamical markings and articulation became more standardized, allowing for more precise interpretation of performance.5. Notation: Music notation became more standardized during the Classical era, with a common set of symbols becoming accepted for representing musical ideas.

During this time, the consciousness of the deceased is said to experience various hallucinations and illusions before finally transitioning into a new life.The intermediate state is considered to be a crucial time for spiritual transformation and is viewed as an opportunity for the individual to break free from the cycle of rebirth and achieve enlightenment. Through meditation and spiritual practice, the individual can navigate the Bardo state and achieve a higher level of consciousness, ultimately leading to liberation from suffering.It is important to note that while the concept of an intermediate state between lives is central to Buddhist belief, the specifics of this state and its duration can vary depending on the Buddhist tradition.

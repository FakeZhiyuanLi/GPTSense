It is difficult to determine what specifically may have been buried by prehistoric people, as burial practices varied greatly depending on the time period, culture, and location. However, some common items that may have been buried with the deceased include:- Personal belongings such as jewelry, tools, and clothing- Offerings such as food, drink, and pottery- Weapons and other items of status or importance- Animal remains, either for ceremonial or practical purposes- Human remains, such as those of ancestors or sacrificial victimsUltimately, the specific burial practices and items buried by prehistoric people are likely to remain a mystery, as these ancient cultures did not leave written records and only a fraction of their artifacts may have survived to modern times.

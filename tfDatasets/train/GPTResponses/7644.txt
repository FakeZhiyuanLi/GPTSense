USB (Universal Serial Bus) is a standardized connection technology for connecting peripheral devices to a computer. It is a plug-and-play interface that allows devices such as keyboards, mice, printers, and external hard drives to be easily connected to a computer. USB ports can also be used to charge devices such as smartphones and tablets. USB has evolved over the years and currently supports several versions including USB 1.0, USB 2.0, USB 3.0, and USB 4.0, each with improved data transfer speeds and capabilities.

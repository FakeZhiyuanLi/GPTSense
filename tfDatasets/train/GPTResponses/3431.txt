It can include bribery, embezzlement, nepotism, cronyism, and other similar activities.Repression of political corruption involves measures taken by governments and other stakeholders to prevent, investigate, prosecute, and punish those engaged in corrupt activities. This can include institutional reforms, such as strengthening anti-corruption laws and institutions, as well as civil society engagement in monitoring and exposing corrupt practices.Effective repression of political corruption requires political will, transparency, and accountability. It is essential for promoting good governance, ensuring that public resources are used for the public good, and strengthening the legitimacy of democratic institutions.

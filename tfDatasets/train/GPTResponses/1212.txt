Mixed race individuals were often discriminated against and kept from participating in many aspects of society, such as:1. Voting: In many countries, mixed race individuals were denied the right to vote or were subject to strict voting requirements that made it difficult for them to have a say in government.2. Education: Mixed race individuals were often excluded from schools and universities, and were denied the opportunity to receive a good education.3. Employment: Discrimination in the workplace was rife, and mixed race individuals were often passed over for jobs in favor of their white counterparts.4. Military service: Mixed race individuals were often excluded from military service or were relegated to positions with lower pay and status.5. Social life: Mixed race individuals were sometimes excluded from social events and were not welcome in certain places like clubs and restaurants.Overall, mixed race individuals experienced significant limits on their opportunities and were often subjected to discrimination and prejudice throughout society.

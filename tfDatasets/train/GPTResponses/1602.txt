The major organs of the immune system are:1. Thymus - produces immune cells called T cells2. Bone marrow - produces immune cells called B cells3. Spleen - filters blood and removes damaged cells and foreign substances4. Lymph nodes - filter lymph fluid and trap foreign substances and harmful cells5. Peyer's patches - immune cells located in the small intestine that protect against harmful substances6. Adenoids and tonsils - lymphoid tissues in the throat that help trap and remove harmful substances breathed in through the nose and mouth.

During different periods of history, there were different rulers of the largest kingdom in Southeast Asia.One of the most significant and powerful kingdoms in Southeast Asia was the Khmer Empire, which existed from the 9th to the 15th century and covered much of modern-day Cambodia, Thailand, Laos, and Vietnam. The Khmer Empire was ruled by a series of kings, including Jayavarman II, Yasovarman I, Suryavarman II, and Jayavarman VII.In modern times, the largest country in Southeast Asia by land area and population is Indonesia, which is ruled by a president and a democratic political system. The Indonesian archipelago has a history of powerful empires, including Majapahit and Srivijaya, which were ruled by monarchs.

Executive agreements, on the other hand, are made solely by the president and are not subject to Senate approval. They are generally used for more routine matters, such as exchanging scientific or technical information.Congressional-executive agreements are made by the president and approved by a simple majority of both the House of Representatives and the Senate. They have the force of domestic law just like treaties.Lastly, sole executive agreements are made by the president alone, without any approval or involvement from Congress. They are generally used for routine matters and have limited legal effect.While all of these agreements are legally binding under international law, they differ in terms of their domestic legal status and approval processes.

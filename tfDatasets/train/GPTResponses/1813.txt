Many species of plants and animals were rarely found after the extreme deforestation between the 17th and 19th centuries. Some of these include:1. Large predators such as wolves, bears, and mountain lions2. Old-growth forests with mature and diverse tree species3. Endangered species such as the ivory-billed woodpecker and Carolina parakeet4. Unique ecosystems such as the Eastern deciduous forest5. Wide-ranging migratory birds that require extensive forest cover for nesting and foraging6. Indigenous communities and their traditional knowledge and practices related to forest conservation and management.

1. Triggers: Allergens or irritants such as pollen, smoke, dust mites, mold, and pet dander can worsen asthma symptoms.2. Weather: Changes in temperature, humidity, and atmospheric pressure can impact asthma symptoms. Cold air, dry air, and changes in temperature can exacerbate symptoms.3. Infections: Respiratory infections like colds, flu, and bronchitis can cause asthma symptoms to worsen.4. Exercise: Physical activity can trigger asthma symptoms in some people, especially when they exercise in cold, dry air.5. Emotions: Emotional stress and anxiety can trigger asthma symptoms.6. Medications: Certain medicines such as aspirin and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) can trigger asthma symptoms.7. Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes during menstruation, pregnancy, and menopause can affect asthma symptoms.8. Genetics: Asthma has a genetic component, and people with a family history of asthma are more likely to experience more severe symptoms.9. Smoking: Smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke can worsen asthma symptoms.10. Other medical conditions: Conditions such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and sinusitis can trigger asthma symptoms.

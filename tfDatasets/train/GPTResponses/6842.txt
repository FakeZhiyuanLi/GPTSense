The law of criminal procedure is made up of several components, including:1. The Constitution: The United States Constitution and state constitutions delineate the basic rights and protections individuals have in criminal proceedings.2. Statutes: Statutes, or written laws, establish the procedures and rules for criminal trials and investigations.3. Case law: Case law, or legal precedent established by court decisions, shapes the application of criminal procedure law.4. Rules of Procedure: Courts establish rules of procedure to govern the conduct of criminal proceedings.5. Administrative regulations: Administrative agencies may also develop regulations that affect criminal procedure, such as rules for conducting investigations or collecting evidence.6. Jury instructions: In criminal trials, judges provide instructions to the jury on how to apply the law to the evidence presented.Together, these components form the law of criminal procedure, which governs the investigation, arrest, prosecution, and defense of individuals accused of committing crimes.

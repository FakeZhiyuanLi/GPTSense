Federal agencies are granted a wide range of powers by Congress, including:1. Executive powers: Congress grants federal agencies the power to execute the laws passed by Congress.2. Rulemaking authority: Federal agencies are granted the power to create rules and regulations that are necessary to enforce the laws passed by Congress.3. Investigatory powers: Federal agencies have the power to conduct investigations to enforce the laws and regulations that they are responsible for enforcing.4. Enforcement powers: Federal agencies have the power to enforce the laws and regulations that they are responsible for enforcing.5. Administrative powers: Federal agencies have the power to manage their own employees, budgets, and resources to carry out their missions.6. International powers: In some cases, federal agencies are granted the power to interact with foreign governments and international organizations to regulate certain areas of activity.

The other aim of the Civil Rights Act was to end discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin in public accommodations, employment, and education. It aimed to provide equal opportunities and protections to all individuals, regardless of their backgrounds. The Act also aimed to promote the integration of schools and to end segregation in public facilities, such as restaurants, hotels, and other businesses that serve the public. Additionally, the Act prohibited the use of federal funds for any program that discriminated against individuals based on their race.

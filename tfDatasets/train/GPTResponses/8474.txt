The end of the 18th century saw the emergence of several new subjects, including:1. Sociology: The study of social behavior, relationships, and institutions.2. Psychology: The study of the human mind and behavior.3. Political economy: The study of the relationships between economics and politics.4. Anthropology: The study of human societies, cultures, and their development.5. Geology: The study of the Earth's physical structure, processes, and history.6. Linguistics: The study of language and its structure, history, and use.7. Botany: The study of plants, including their structure, growth, and classification.8. Chemistry: The study of matter and its properties, structure, and reactions.9. Zoology: The study of animals and their behavior, physiology, and classification.

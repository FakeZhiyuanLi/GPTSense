Native copper was extracted with primitive tools between 800 and 1600 in various locations around the world, including:1. North America - particularly in the Great Lakes region, including Michigan's Keweenaw Peninsula.2. South America - in the Andes Mountains region of Chile, Peru, and Bolivia.3. Europe - in parts of the Balkans, including Serbia and Bulgaria.4. Africa - particularly in Zambia and the Democratic Republic of Congo.5. Asia - in areas of Iran and Uzbekistan.These regions were known for their rich copper deposits and were mined by indigenous peoples using stone tools and fire-setting methods.

There were various architectural influences in the medieval period. Some of the prominent ones include:1. Romanesque architecture: This style was prevalent from the 8th to the 12th century and was characterized by features like rounded arches, barrel vaults, and thick walls.2. Gothic architecture: This style emerged in the 12th century and continued till the 16th century. Gothic architecture was characterized by features like pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses.3. Byzantine architecture: This style emerged in the 5th century and was characterized by features like domes, pendentives, and mosaics.4. Norman architecture: This style emerged in the 11th century and was characterized by features like rounded arches, decorative arcading, and chevron motifs.5. Moorish architecture: This style was prevalent in North Africa and the Iberian Peninsula and was characterized by features like horseshoe arches, intricate geometric patterns, and Arabic script.

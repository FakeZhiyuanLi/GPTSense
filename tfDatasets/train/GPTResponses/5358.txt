The typical range that Prussian guns could fire varied depending on the type of gun. In the mid-19th century, Prussian artillery was divided into light, field, and heavy artillery.Light artillery consisted of smaller guns that could fire up to 2,000 meters (2,187 yards), while field artillery, which included the famous Krupp 12-pounder cannon, had a range of up to 4,000 meters (4,374 yards).Heavy artillery, such as the Krupp 21-centimeter (8.3-inch) howitzer, could fire up to 12,000 meters (13,123 yards) and was used to destroy fortifications and other fortified positions.

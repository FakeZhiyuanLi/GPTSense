The 1912 exhibition of cubism, also known as the Salon de la Section d'Or, showcased the works of artists who were associated with the Cubist movement. The exhibition included works by prominent artists such as Pablo Picasso, Georges Braque, Juan Gris, and Francis Picabia. The exhibit aimed to showcase the cubist style and to highlight its importance in modern art. It featured artworks that showcased the use of geometric shapes, fragmented forms, and the exploration of space and perspective. The exhibition was significant in the development of the cubist movement and was a milestone in the history of modern art.

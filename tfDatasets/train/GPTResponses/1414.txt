There are several groups and institutions that still use Latin:1. Eastern Catholic Churches: These are churches that are in communion with the Roman Catholic Church but have their own traditions and liturgies. Some of these churches use Latin in their liturgy alongside their native language.2. Anglican Church: Some Anglican churches use parts of the Latin liturgy in their services, particularly the Book of Common Prayer.3. Traditional Latin Mass: Some Roman Catholic priests and communities still celebrate the Traditional Latin Mass, also known as the Tridentine Mass.4. Education: Latin is still taught in some schools and universities as a classical language.5. Juridical and academic institutions: Latin is used as the official language in various international institutions, such as the European Union and the United Nations. Many legal and scientific terms are also written in Latin.6. Music: Latin is still used in choral and classical music compositions.

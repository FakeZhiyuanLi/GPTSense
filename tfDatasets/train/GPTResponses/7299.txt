Burma (also known as Myanmar) was involved with several countries in 2015, but some of the major ones were China, India, and the United States. In particular, China and India have been key trading partners with Burma and have invested heavily in the country's infrastructure, while the United States has been more involved in promoting democracy and human rights in the country. Additionally, Burma has been engaged in peace talks with various ethnic groups within the country, as well as neighboring countries like Thailand and Bangladesh.

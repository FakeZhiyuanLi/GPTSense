Organizations use various methods to study corruption, including:1. Interviews with whistleblowers or informants who have firsthand knowledge of corrupt activities.2. Analysis of financial records and transactions to identify suspicious patterns or anomalies.3. Transparency International's Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI), an annual ranking of countries based on perceived levels of corruption.4. Corruption Risk Assessments (CRAs) that identify specific areas or processes within an organization that are vulnerable to corruption.5. Surveillance and monitoring of key individuals or entities suspected of engaging in corrupt activities.

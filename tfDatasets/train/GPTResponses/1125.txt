The coding format for audio can vary depending on the specific type of audio being encoded. There are many different audio coding formats used for various purposes, such as:- MP3: a popular lossy audio compression format used for music and other audio applications- WAV: a versatile uncompressed audio format commonly used for high-quality audio recordings and editing- AAC: a highly efficient lossy audio compression format used for streaming and mobile applications- FLAC: a lossless audio compression format used for preserving high-quality audio recordings while reducing file size- OGG: a free, open-source lossy audio compression format used for streaming and other applicationsThere are many other audio coding formats as well, each with their own strengths and weaknesses and intended uses.

The authors of Regimen Sanitatis Salernitanum are unknown, but it is believed that it was compiled by a group of physicians and scholars who resided at the School of Salerno in Italy during the 12th century. The school was a renowned center for medical education and research during the Middle Ages, attracting scholars from all over Europe. The authors of the text were likely influenced by the school's emphasis on a holistic approach to medicine, which combined the principles of ancient Greek and Roman medicine with Christian and Islamic traditions.

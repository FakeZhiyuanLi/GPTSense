There were several laws that were introduced to support Blacks including:1. The Civil Rights Act of 1964 - This law prohibited discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, or national origin in employment, voting, and public accommodations.2. The Voting Rights Act of 1965 - This law eliminated discriminatory voting practices such as literacy tests and poll taxes that were used to prevent African-Americans from exercising their right to vote.3. The Fair Housing Act - This law prohibited housing discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.4. The Equal Pay Act - This law mandated equal pay for equal work regardless of race, gender, or other factors.5. The Americans with Disabilities Act - This law prohibited discrimination against people with disabilities in employment, transportation, public accommodations, and telecommunications.6. The Age Discrimination in Employment Act - This law prohibited employment discrimination against workers over the age of 40.These laws were instrumental in improving the lives of African-Americans and promoting equality in the United States.

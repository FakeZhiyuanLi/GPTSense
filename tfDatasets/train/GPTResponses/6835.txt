Monasteries often cultivated a variety of plants such as herbs, vegetables, flowers, and fruit trees. Some commonly grown plants include:1. Medicinal herbs such as chamomile, lavender, and mint2. Culinary herbs such as basil, rosemary, and thyme3. Vegetables such as beans, peas, and root vegetables4. Flowers such as roses, lilies, and marigolds for decoration and fragrance5. Fruit trees such as apple, pear, and cherry for jams, jellies, and preserves.Monks and nuns also grew medicinal plants for use in their infirmaries and to create tinctures, ointments, and salves. The cultivation of plants played a significant role in their self-sustainability and in providing food and medicine for their communities.

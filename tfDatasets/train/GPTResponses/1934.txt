Corruption that is public refers to actions or practices conducted by government officials or public servants in violation of their duties, often for personal gain or alternatively as a result of systemic or cultural issues within the government. This might include acts of bribery, embezzlement, or influence peddling.Private corruption refers to actions or practices by individuals or non-governmental entities, such as businesses or non-profit organizations, to advance their interests through illegal or unethical means. This could include bribery of public officials, fraud or kickbacks, or other deceptive or manipulative practices. Private corruption is typically less regulated than public corruption, but can still have serious consequences for social and economic development.

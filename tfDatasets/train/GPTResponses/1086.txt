The Russian Empire used various justifications for its territorial conquests, including:1. The idea of "Manifest Destiny" - the belief that it was Russia's destiny to expand and colonize new territories.2. The need for "buffer zones" - the idea that the empire needed to create a buffer zone of friendly states around its borders to protect against potential enemies.3. The belief in the "superiority" of Russian culture - the idea that it was Russia's duty to "civilize" inferior cultures and bring them under its rule.4. Religious motives - the belief that it was Russia's duty to spread Orthodox Christianity to non-Christian regions.5. Economic motives - the desire to acquire resources and expand trade networks.Overall, the Russian Empire's expansion was driven by a combination of geopolitical, economic, cultural, and ideological factors.

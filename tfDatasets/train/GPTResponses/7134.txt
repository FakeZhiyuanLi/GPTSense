1. Geometric shapes and forms: Cubism is characterized by the use of simple geometric shapes such as cubes, rectangles, triangles, and circles to represent objects and figures.2. Fragmentation: Objects and figures are often fragmented or broken down into smaller pieces or facets and then reassembled to create a sense of multiple viewpoints.3. Multiple perspectives: Cubism often portrays multiple viewpoints of the same object or figure within a single composition, emphasizing the complexity and subjectivity of visual perception.4. Limited color palette: Cubist works often feature a limited color palette, with muted or earthy colors prevailing to create a sense of unity and harmony within the composition.

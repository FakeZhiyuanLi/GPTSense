Several acts were passed relating to the FBI after 9/11, including:1. USA PATRIOT Act (Uniting and Strengthening America by Providing Appropriate Tools Required to Intercept and Obstruct Terrorism Act): This act increased the powers of law enforcement agencies, including the FBI, to collect and share intelligence information to prevent terrorist attacks.2. Homeland Security Act: This act created the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and placed the FBI under its jurisdiction.3. Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act: This act established the Office of the Director of National Intelligence and created the National Counterterrorism Center to coordinate the sharing of intelligence among various agencies, including the FBI.4. FISA Amendments Act: This act amended the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) to allow for increased surveillance of non-U.S. citizens suspected of involvement in terrorism.5. USA Freedom Act: This act modified certain provisions of the USA PATRIOT Act, including the bulk collection of phone metadata by the FBI.

An earthworm's digestive system consists of the following organs:1. Mouth: It is located at the anterior end of the earthworm and is used for ingestion of food.2. Pharynx: This muscular tube-like structure helps to suck the food into the digestive tract.3. Esophagus: This is a short tube that connects the pharynx to the crop.4. Crop: This is a temporary storage area for food.5. Gizzard: This is a muscular and thick-walled structure that grinds the food using sand grains and small stones present in the soil.6. Intestine: This is a narrow and long tube lined with numerous folds that help in absorption of digested food.7. Anus: It is located at the posterior end of the earthworm and is used for elimination of waste material.

There were several wars fought between the Seleucid Empire and Ptolemaic Egypt during the Hellenistic period, including:1. Syrian Wars (274-168 BC): These were a series of conflicts fought between the Seleucid Empire and Ptolemaic Egypt over control of the Syrian region. The wars began in 274 BC and lasted almost a century, with both sides winning and losing battles.2. Battle of Raphia (217 BC): This was a major battle fought between the Seleucid Empire and Ptolemaic Egypt on the borders of Palestine. Ptolemy IV of Egypt defeated the Seleucid army of Antiochus III, securing Egypt's control over the region.3. Fourth Syrian War (219-217 BC): This was another conflict between the two empires, which ended with the Battle of Raphia.4. Sixth Syrian War (170-168 BC): This was the last war fought between the Seleucid Empire and Ptolemaic Egypt. Egypt emerged victorious and gained control over the Seleucid territories in the Levant.Overall, the Seleucid Empire and Ptolemaic Egypt were rivals and often fought over control of key regions in the eastern Mediterranean. The wars between the two empires had a significant impact on the politics and culture of the Hellenistic period.

There are several factors that are known to weaken the prevention of asthma:1. Exposure to tobacco smoke: Exposure to tobacco smoke increases the risk of developing asthma and can make existing asthma symptoms worse.2. Poor air quality: Environmental factors such as air pollution, dust, and pollen can trigger asthma symptoms and make it difficult to prevent asthma attacks.3. Lack of physical activity: Physical activity is important in maintaining overall health, and lack of exercise can weaken the immune system, making it easier to develop asthma.4. Poor diet: A diet lacking in fruits, vegetables, and other nutrient-rich foods can weaken the immune system and increase the risk of asthma.5. Stress: Stress can weaken the immune system and trigger asthma attacks in some individuals.It is important to address these factors and make lifestyle changes to prevent the development of asthma and manage the symptoms in those who already have the condition.

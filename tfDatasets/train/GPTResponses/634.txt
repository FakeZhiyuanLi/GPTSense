An important guiding principle of Buddhist practice is the Four Noble Truths. These truths are:1. Suffering (dukkha) exists in life2. Suffering is caused by craving and attachment3. Suffering can be overcome4. The Eightfold Path is the way to overcome sufferingThe Eightfold Path includes the following practices: right understanding, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These practices are intended to help individuals develop wisdom, ethical conduct, and mental discipline, ultimately leading to enlightenment and the cessation of suffering.

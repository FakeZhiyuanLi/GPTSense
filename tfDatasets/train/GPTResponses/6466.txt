The chivalry of British constitutional monarchy was established over time by various monarchs and their courtiers. However, the principle of chivalry has its roots in medieval Europe, where it was a code of conduct for knights and nobles. The chivalric ideals of honor, bravery, and loyalty were adopted by many monarchs and became a central part of the British monarchy's self-image. Some notable monarchs who helped to establish the chivalry of the British constitutional monarchy include King Arthur, Edward III, Henry V, and Queen Elizabeth I. Additionally, the concept was reinforced during the reign of Queen Victoria, who embodied the chivalric ideals of duty, discipline, and sacrifice.

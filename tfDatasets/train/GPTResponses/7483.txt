The Society of Friends, also known as Quakers, had several goals, including promoting equality, justice, peace, and simplicity. They believed in living a simple and unadorned life, treating all people equally, and advocating for social reform, including the abolition of slavery and the rights of women. They also prioritized their spiritual beliefs, emphasizing a direct relationship with God and the importance of listening for divine guidance. Overall, their goal was to create a more just and peaceful world through their actions and beliefs.

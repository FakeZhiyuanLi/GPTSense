Harold L. Ickes held several positions of power during his political career. He served as Secretary of the Interior under President Franklin D. Roosevelt from 1933 to 1946, overseeing the management and conservation of natural resources and public lands. He was also a key advisor to Roosevelt and helped shape New Deal policies. Additionally, Ickes served as the Chairman of the Public Works Administration and played a significant role in the construction of public works projects, such as dams and highways, during the Great Depression.

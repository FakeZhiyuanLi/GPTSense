Congress authorized the Federal Reserve banks to perform several functions, which include:1. Conducting open market operations: The Federal Reserve banks are authorized to buy and sell government securities in the open market to influence the supply of credit and money.2. Lending money to banks: The Federal Reserve banks can lend money to member banks to help them meet short-term liquidity needs.3. Setting reserve requirements: The Federal Reserve banks can establish reserve requirements for member banks, which specify the percentage of deposits that banks must hold in reserve.4. Supervising banks: The Federal Reserve banks have supervisory powers over member banks to ensure their safety and soundness.5. Issuing currency: The Federal Reserve banks have the power to issue and retire currency as needed to meet the demands of the economy.6. Providing payment services: The Federal Reserve banks provide payment services to depository institutions, including clearing checks and handling electronic transactions.

The Gregorian calendar reform had several parts, including:1. The length of the year was changed from 365.25 days to 365.2425 days by skipping leap years on centennial years that are not divisible by 400.2. The leap day was moved from February 24 to February 29.3. The start of the year was changed from March 25 to January 1.4. The date of the equinox was fixed on March 21 instead of March 11.Overall, the reform aimed to adjust the calendar to more accurately reflect the length of a year and the position of the Earth in its orbit.

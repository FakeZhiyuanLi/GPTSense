After defeating the British army, George Washington also played a significant role in the establishment of the United States as a nation. He was elected as the first president of the United States and served two terms from 1789 to 1797. During his presidency, he worked towards the formation of a strong national government and played a crucial role in formulating policies and laws. He also worked towards maintaining neutrality and peaceful relations with other countries. After his presidency, Washington returned to his estate, Mount Vernon, where he continued to oversee his plantations until his death in 1799.

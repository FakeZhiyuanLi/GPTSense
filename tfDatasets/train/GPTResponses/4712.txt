The Great Plains is a large area encompassing the central portion of North America, stretching from the Rocky Mountains in the west to the Mississippi River in the east. It is characterized by its flat terrain, semi-arid climate, and grassland prairies that are home to diverse plant and animal species. The Great Plains are an important agricultural region, producing crops such as wheat, corn, and soybeans, and are also home to oil and gas reserves. The area is also steeped in history with important events such as the Indian Wars and the Dust Bowl, playing significant roles in shaping the region's culture and identity.

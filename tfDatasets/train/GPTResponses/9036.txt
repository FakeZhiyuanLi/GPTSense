It is subjective to determine Russia's largest regional rival as it can depend on various factors and perspectives. However, some of the regional rivals that Russia has faced or is facing include:1. NATO: Russia considers NATO as a military alliance that poses a threat to its national security.2. Ukraine: Russia's annexation of Crimea and its support for separatist rebels in eastern Ukraine has led to tensions and conflict between the two countries.3. Georgia: Russia's invasion of Georgia in 2008 and its continued support for separatist rebels in Abkhazia and South Ossetia has strained relations between the two countries.4. Turkey: Russia's involvement in the Syrian conflict and its support for the Assad government has put it at odds with Turkey, which supports the opposition.5. China: While Russia and China have formed a strategic partnership, they have also competed for influence in Central Asia and the Asia-Pacific region.

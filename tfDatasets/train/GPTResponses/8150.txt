The scientific revolution refers to a time period from the 16th to 18th century where there was a fundamental shift in the way people viewed and understood the natural world. During this time, new scientific discoveries were made and theories were developed that challenged traditional beliefs and ideas. The scientific method was developed, which emphasized the importance of experimentation, observation, and empirical evidence. This period was marked by the work of notable scientists such as Galileo, Newton, and Copernicus, among others, who made important contributions to the fields of physics, astronomy, biology, and chemistry. The scientific revolution had an enormous impact on society and influenced the way people thought about the world and their place in it.

Bermuda is an archipelago composed of about 180 small islands, islets, and rocks in the North Atlantic Ocean. Some of the natural features that make up Bermuda include:1. Coral reefs that surround the islands2. Pink sand beaches such as Horseshoe Bay and Elbow Beach3. Limestone cliffs and rock formations along the coast4. Marshes, mangroves, and tidal flats that provide habitats for bird and marine life5. Subtropical forests and vegetation, with species such as palmetto trees, cedars, and Bermuda olivewood6. Natural caves and underground lakes such as the Crystal Caves and the Blue Hole7. Natural harbors and inlets, such as Hamilton Harbor and St. George's Harbor, which have played a significant role in Bermuda's history and economy.

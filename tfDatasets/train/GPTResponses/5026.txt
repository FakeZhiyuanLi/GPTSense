The Census of Antarctic Marine Life was a study that aimed to document the biodiversity and distribution of marine life in the Southern Ocean surrounding Antarctica. The study involved collaboration between more than a thousand scientists from over 30 countries and utilized various methods such as remotely operated vehicles, underwater cameras, and genetic analysis to survey the region. The findings from the study provided valuable insights into the complex and interconnected ecosystems of the Southern Ocean and highlighted the need for continued research and conservation efforts in the region.

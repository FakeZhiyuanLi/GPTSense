There are several classes of drugs that have been proven to reduce the risk of death and other events in people with high blood pressure, including:1. Angiotensin converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors: such as lisinopril, enalapril, ramipril, etc.2. Angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs): such as losartan, valsartan, candesartan, etc.3. Calcium channel blockers (CCBs): such as amlodipine, nifedipine, diltiazem, etc.4. Beta-blockers: such as metoprolol, atenolol, propranolol, etc.5. Diuretics: such as hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone, furosemide, etc.These drugs work by different mechanisms to lower blood pressure and protect the heart and blood vessels from damage. They have been shown to reduce the risk of heart attack, stroke, heart failure, and other cardiovascular events in people with high blood pressure. The choice of drug or combination of drugs will depend on the individual's condition and medical history, and should be determined by a healthcare professional.

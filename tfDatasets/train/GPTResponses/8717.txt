Austria felt threatened by different countries at different times throughout history. Some examples include:- During the 19th century, Austria felt threatened by the nationalist and liberal movements in its own empire, as well as by the rising power of Prussia in the German-speaking world.- During World War I, Austria-Hungary felt threatened by Serbia and its allies, leading to the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand and the subsequent declaration of war.- During World War II, Austria was annexed by Nazi Germany and felt threatened by the Allied powers, who eventually liberated the country in 1945.- In the post-World War II era, Austria aligned itself with the West and felt threatened by the Soviet Union and its Communist allies in Eastern Europe.

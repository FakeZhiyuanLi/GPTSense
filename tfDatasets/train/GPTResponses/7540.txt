Barbershop music is a type of a cappella music specific to American culture. It originated in the late 19th and early 20th centuries and is characterized by four-part harmony with a lead melody, tenor harmony, baritone harmony, and bass harmony. Barbershop music has a distinctive sound and often features intricate chord progressions, vocal embellishments, and tight harmonies. It is often performed by quartets or choruses and has been popularized through competitions, performances, and recordings.

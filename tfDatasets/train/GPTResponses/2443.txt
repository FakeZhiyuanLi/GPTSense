One example of a notable decorated book with a binding of jewel-encrusted gold is the Lindau Gospels. The Lindau Gospels is a 9th-century illuminated manuscript that contains the four Gospels of the New Testament. It is named after Lindau, a town in Germany where it is currently held in the collection of the Stiftsbibliothek.The binding of the Lindau Gospels is made of gold and is encrusted with precious gems such as garnets, pearls, and sapphires. It features a large crucifix on the front cover and an image of the four evangelists on the back cover. The intricate designs on the binding are believed to have been created by the same artist who illuminated the manuscript.The Lindau Gospels is considered one of the finest examples of Carolingian art and is highly regarded for its exceptional craftsmanship and beauty.

Horner's method is frequently mentioned in algebra and calculus textbooks when discussing polynomial functions. It is often included as a technique for polynomial evaluation or for finding roots of polynomials. Some examples of textbooks that include Horner's method are "Elementary Algebra" by Harold Jacobs, "Calculus: Early Transcendentals" by James Stewart, and "Algebra and Trigonometry: Functions and Applications" by Paul A. Foerster.

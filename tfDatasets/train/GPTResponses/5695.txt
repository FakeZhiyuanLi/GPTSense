Queen Victoria's reign was considered the Victorian era, which lasted from 1837 to 1901. It was a period of significant social, political, and economic change in Britain, characterized by industrialization, urbanization, the expansion of the British Empire, and the rise of a middle class. The era also saw major reforms in areas such as education, healthcare, and labor laws, as well as the growth of the arts and the development of new technologies. Queen Victoria's reign is often seen as a symbol of stability and progress in a time of rapid change.

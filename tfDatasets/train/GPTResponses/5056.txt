All India Radio (AIR) is the national public radio broadcaster of India. It is a division of the Prasar Bharati, which is a statutory autonomous body established under the Prasar Bharati Act and broadcasts in 23 languages and 146 dialects. With over 450 radio stations covering almost 95 percent of India's geographic area, it is one of the largest radio networks in the world. AIR provides news, current affairs, music, and entertainment programs across various genres to listeners across the country. It was established in 1930 and played a crucial role in the freedom movement of India by broadcasting speeches and messages of national leaders like Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru.

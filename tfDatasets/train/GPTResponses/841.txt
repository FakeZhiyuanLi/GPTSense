Alfred North Whitehead was highly educated in philosophy, having studied the subject extensively both as a student and as a professor. He received his undergraduate degree in mathematics from Trinity College, Cambridge, and then went on to study philosophy with G. E. Moore and Bertrand Russell. He also attended lectures by the German philosopher Gottlob Frege while studying in Germany.Whitehead went on to teach philosophy at both Cambridge and Harvard Universities, and he was highly respected as a philosopher in his own right. He made major contributions to metaphysics, logic, and the philosophy of science, and he was a key figure in the development of the philosophy known as process philosophy. Overall, Whitehead was widely regarded as one of the most important philosophers of the 20th century.

Alexandria was known for being a major center of Hellenistic culture and trade in the ancient world. It was founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC and became a hub for commerce and maritime transport, serving as a link between Europe, Asia, and Africa. The city was also renowned for its famous Library of Alexandria, which housed a vast collection of manuscripts and was considered the foremost repository of knowledge in the ancient world. Alexandria was also a center of learning, attracting scholars and philosophers from all over the Mediterranean world. In addition, the city was known for its lighthouse, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, which served as a guiding beacon for ships navigating the Mediterranean Sea.

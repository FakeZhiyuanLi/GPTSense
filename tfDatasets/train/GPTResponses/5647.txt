Sexual identity refers to an individual's perception of their own gender and attraction to others, either as heterosexual, homosexual, bisexual, or asexual. On the other hand, sexual behavior refers to the actions or behaviors that an individual engages in when expressing their sexual desires or preferences, including the type of sexual activities or partners they seek.In other words, sexual identity is about an individual's internal and often personal perception of their own sexual orientation or preferences, while sexual behavior is a reflection of the actions and activities they engage in to express and satisfy those preferences.

The Romans used a variety of clocks, including:- Water clocks or clepsydras, which used the regulated flow of water through a container to measure time.- Sun clocks, also known as sundials, which were used to track time based on the movement of the sun's shadow.- Candle clocks, which used marked candles that burned at a known rate to measure time.- Mechanical clocks, which were invented in the 14th century and were not used by the ancient Romans.

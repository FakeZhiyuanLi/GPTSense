Exposure to various compounds can trigger asthma. Some of the common compounds include:1. Pollen and other environmental allergens2. Smoke from tobacco, wood or other sources3. Chemical irritants such as cleaning products, perfumes, and paints4. Air pollution and industrial fumes5. Cold air or changes in temperature and humidity6. Certain medications such as aspirin and beta-blockers7. Foods and food additives, such as sulfites and artificial sweeteners8. Stress and strong emotions such as laughter, crying or anger9. Animals and their dander or urine10. Exercise-induced asthma, where physical activity triggers asthma symptoms.

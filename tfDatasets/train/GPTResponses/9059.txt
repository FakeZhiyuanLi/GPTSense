In the 18th century, federalism referred to a political system in which power was divided between a central government and individual states or provinces that retained some degree of autonomy. This system was common in countries like the United States, where the federal government shared power with individual states.Confederalism, on the other hand, referred to a political system in which power was decentralized and individual states or provinces held most of the political power. This system was common in countries like Switzerland, where individual cantons retained significant autonomy over internal affairs while the national government was responsible for external affairs.

Norbulingka is a palace and park located in Lhasa, Tibet Autonomous Region, China. It is known as the Dalai Lama's summer palace and served as the traditional residence of the Dalai Lamas until the 14th Dalai Lama. The palace complex includes several pavilions and gardens, which are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Today, Norbulingka is open to the public and serves as a popular tourist destination.

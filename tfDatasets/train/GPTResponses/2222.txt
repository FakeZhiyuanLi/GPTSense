During the Baroque era, several things became more common:1. Ornamentation: Baroque art and architecture were filled with ornamental details like curved lines, intricate patterns, and colorful designs.2. Counterpoint: The use of multiple melodies playing simultaneously became more common in Baroque music, resulting in a rich and complex sound.3. Opera: The Baroque era saw the rise of opera as a popular form of entertainment. Composers like Handel and Vivaldi wrote operas that combined music, drama, and spectacle.4. Improvisation: Many Baroque musicians were skilled improvisers, adding their own flourishes and embellishments to the music.5. Dramatic contrasts: Baroque art and music often featured bold contrasts between light and dark, loud and soft, and major and minor keys. This helped create a sense of drama and tension in the works.

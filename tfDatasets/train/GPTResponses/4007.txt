Hoover used various tools and methods to identify and capture bootleggers during his time as the director of the Bureau of Investigation (later known as the FBI), including:1. Informants: Hoover recruited informants to gather intelligence on bootleggers and their criminal activities.2. Wiretapping: Hoover authorized wiretapping of suspected bootleggers' phone lines to intercept their conversations and gather evidence.3. Prohibition agents: Hoover employed a team of prohibition agents to investigate and prosecute bootleggers.4. Surveillance: Hoover's agents conducted surveillance operations, both in person and by air, to monitor and track bootleggers and their operations.5. Raids: Hoover's agents conducted numerous raids on bootlegging operations, seizing illegal alcohol and arresting individuals involved in the trade.

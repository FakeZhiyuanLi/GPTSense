1. Allergies: People with asthma are more likely to have allergies, which can trigger asthma symptoms. Common allergens include dust mites, pollen, and pet dander.2. Sinusitis: Asthma and sinusitis (inflammation of the sinuses) often occur together. Symptoms of sinusitis include congestion, facial pain, and headaches.3. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD): People with asthma are more likely to have GERD, a condition in which stomach acid backs up into the esophagus. GERD can worsen asthma symptoms and make it more difficult to control asthma.

There have been numerous individuals and groups throughout history who have attempted or desired to take over Constantinople (now Istanbul), including:1. Persians: The Sassanid Persian Empire, under the rule of King Khosrow II, laid siege to Constantinople in 626 AD but failed to take the city.2. Arabs: The Arab-Berber Muslim armies of the Umayyad Caliphate attempted several attacks on Constantinople in the 7th and 8th centuries, but they were unable to capture the city.3. Vikings: In the 9th century, Viking raiders, led by Harald Hardrada, launched a failed attack on Constantinople.4. Crusaders: During the Fourth Crusade (1202-1204), a group of Western Europeans led by the Venetians captured and sacked Constantinople, taking control of the Byzantine Empire.5. Ottomans: The Ottoman Turks, led by Mehmed II, captured Constantinople in 1453, ending the Byzantine Empire and establishing the Ottoman Empire.

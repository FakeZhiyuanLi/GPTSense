The topic of rupa jhanas is discussed extensively in various Buddhist texts, including the Pali Canon, the Visuddhimagga, and various commentaries by notable scholars and practitioners.One prominent text that addresses the rupa jhanas is the Visuddhimagga (The Path of Purification), written by the Theravada Buddhist scholar and commentary writer Buddhaghosa in the 5th century CE. This text offers a comprehensive guide to various aspects of Buddhist practice, including the jhanas, or states of deep concentration.Additionally, the Sutta Pitaka of the Pali Canon contains various discourses in which the Buddha describes his own experiences with the jhanas, including the rupa jhanas. Some examples of these texts include the Jhana Sutta (AN 9.36) and the Maha-Saccaka Sutta (MN 36).Overall, texts exploring the rupa jhanas can be found in a variety of sources and translations, many of which offer rich insights into the practice of Buddhist meditation and its relationship to the broader path of liberation from suffering.

There have been numerous languages that have been banned from printing on books throughout history, including but not limited to:- Hebrew during the Middle Ages in various parts of Europe due to religious persecution- Welsh during the 16th century by the English monarchy as a means of suppressing Welsh culture- Irish Gaelic during the 17th and 18th centuries by the English government as a means of undermining Irish nationalism- Native American languages during the 19th and early 20th centuries in the United States as part of efforts to assimilate indigenous populations- Kurdish during the 20th century in Turkey as a means of suppressing Kurdish identity and culture- Russian during the Soviet era, as the government sought to control the dissemination of information and suppress dissenting opinions.

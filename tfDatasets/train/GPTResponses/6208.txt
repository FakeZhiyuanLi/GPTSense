After the war, Napoleon focused on consolidating his power as the Emperor of France and building a strong centralized state. He introduced a series of domestic reforms such as the Napoleonic Code, which established a standardized system of laws and greatly influenced civil law codes around the world. He also reorganized the educational system and established a centralized administration, which helped to modernize France and make it more efficient. Additionally, Napoleon expanded the French Empire through conquests and alliances across Europe and beyond.

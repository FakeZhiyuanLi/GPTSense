Yes, Burma (now Myanmar) was affected by both World War I and World War II.During World War I, Burma was a British colony and contributed troops to fight in the war. Many Burmese soldiers were sent to fight in European and Middle Eastern theaters of the war. Additionally, the war disrupted trade and caused economic hardship for many Burmese people.During World War II, Burma was invaded by Japanese forces in 1942. The fighting lasted for about four years, and the country was heavily impacted by the war. Many people were killed, and cities and towns were destroyed. The Burmese National Army fought alongside Allied forces to push the Japanese out of Burma. After the war, Burma gained its independence from Britain in 1948.

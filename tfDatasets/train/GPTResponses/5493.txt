The Constitution retained various key features, including:1. Separation of powers: The Constitution established three separate branches of government - the legislative, executive, and judicial - to prevent any one branch from gaining too much power.2. Federalism: The Constitution established a federal system of government in which power is divided between the national government and the states.3. Bill of Rights: The first ten amendments to the Constitution, known as the Bill of Rights, guarantee individual liberties and limit the power of the federal government.4. Popular sovereignty: The Constitution establishes that the people are the ultimate source of governmental power.5. Representative democracy: The Constitution establishes a system of representative democracy, in which citizens elect representatives to make decisions on their behalf.

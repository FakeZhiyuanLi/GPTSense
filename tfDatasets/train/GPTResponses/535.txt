The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA) requires:1. Protection for service members' rights while they are on active duty2. Relief from certain legal and financial obligations while on active duty, including:- Stopping or delaying civil court proceedings- Suspending certain contractual obligations (such as rental agreements, mortgages, and automobile leases)- Capping interest rates on existing loans- Preventing foreclosure or repossession of property without a court order3. Protection against eviction from rental property, except in certain circumstances4. Continuation of certain insurance policies, such as life insurance, health insurance, and automobile insurance5. Termination of certain contracts, such as cell phone and internet service, without penalty when the service member is called to active duty6. Protection against employment discrimination based on military service.Overall, the SCRA provides legal and financial protections for service members and their families during their time of service.

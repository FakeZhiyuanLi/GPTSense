The changing nature of the meaning of the term "literature" can be described as a dynamic concept that adapts to the evolving cultural, social, and political landscapes of different societies and historical periods. The definition of literature has expanded over time to encompass a wider range of texts, including non-fiction, graphic novels, and oral traditions. Additionally, the criteria used to assess literary value have diversified, reflecting changing attitudes to gender, ethnicity, and other forms of difference. Overall, the meaning of "literature" has become more inclusive, pluralistic, and contextualized, reflecting the complexity of human experience in different times and places.

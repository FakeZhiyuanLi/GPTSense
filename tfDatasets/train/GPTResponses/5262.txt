There were many causes of deaths before World War II, including:1. Diseases: Many people died due to diseases like tuberculosis, smallpox, malaria, cholera, and influenza.2. Natural disasters: Natural disasters like earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, and droughts caused many deaths.3. War and conflicts: There were several wars and conflicts that caused countless deaths before World War II. The Spanish Civil War, the Sino-Japanese War, and the Italo-Ethiopian War are some examples.4. Famine: Several regions of the world faced famine due to poor crop harvests, leading to deaths from starvation.5. Industrial accidents: Industrial accidents like mine collapses and explosions caused many deaths, especially in the early days of the Industrial Revolution.6. Poverty: Many people died due to poverty, lack of access to healthcare, and poor living conditions.

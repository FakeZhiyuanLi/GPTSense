Landmark architecture is defined as a significant or iconic building or structure that has regional, national, or international importance. It is usually a structure that carries historical, cultural, or architectural value and is recognized for its uniqueness or innovation. Examples of landmark architecture include the Eiffel Tower, the Empire State Building, the Sydney Opera House, the Taj Mahal, and the Great Wall of China. These buildings are often recognizable and serve as symbols of the location or country they represent.

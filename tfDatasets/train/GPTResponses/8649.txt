Oklahoma has several species of deer that are found in large populations, including:1. White-tailed deer: This is the most common deer species found in Oklahoma and is distributed throughout the state. There are estimated to be over 500,000 white-tailed deer in Oklahoma.2. Mule deer: This species is found in the western region of Oklahoma and has a population of around 35,000.3. Elk: Elk were reintroduced to Oklahoma in the 1990s and are now found in the southwestern part of the state. The population is estimated to be around 1,000.4. Black-tailed deer: This deer species is found in the southeastern part of Oklahoma and has a relatively small population.

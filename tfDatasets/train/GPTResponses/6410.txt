The notion of sexual orientation in the West refers to an individual's enduring pattern of romantic and sexual attraction towards others of the same gender, opposite gender, or both genders. It is a term used to describe a person's natural inclination or preference for a particular gender as a sexual partner or romantic partner. Sexual orientation is generally considered to be an integral part of an individual's identity and is typically based on a combination of biological, psychological, and social factors. In the West, sexual orientation has become widely recognized as an important aspect of human diversity and has been the subject of significant legal and political debate.

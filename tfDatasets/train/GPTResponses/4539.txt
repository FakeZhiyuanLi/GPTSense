There are several languages that have provided loanwords to English that are still used today. Here are a few examples:1. Latin - many English words have Latin roots, such as "communicate," "telephone," and "television."2. French - after the Norman Conquest of England in 1066, French became the language of the ruling class, and many French words found their way into English. Examples include "restaurant," "chef," and "cuisine."3. German - German has also contributed to English, with words such as "kindergarten," "wanderlust," and "doppelganger."4. Greek - many scientific and medical terms in English come from Greek, such as "biology," "psychology," and "philosophy."5. Spanish - due to the influence of the Spanish empire, several Spanish words have made their way into English, including "siesta," "patio," and "armada."

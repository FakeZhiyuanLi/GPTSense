Some medications that can trigger asthma symptoms include:1. Beta-blockers (used to treat high blood pressure, heart disease, and migraines)2. Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as aspirin and ibuprofen3. ACE inhibitors (used to treat heart disease and high blood pressure)4. Sulfites (used as a preservative in foods and medications)5. Antibiotics such as penicillin and sulfa drugsIt is important to inform your healthcare provider of any medication allergies or adverse reactions you have had in the past to prevent medication-induced asthma symptoms.

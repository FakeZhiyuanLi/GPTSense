There are many places where police have experienced violent problems with protesters. Some notable examples include:1. Hong Kong: In 2019, protests broke out in Hong Kong over a proposed extradition law. The protests escalated into violent clashes between police and protesters, with both sides using tear gas, rubber bullets, and other forms of force.2. United States: The United States has seen numerous protests turn violent, particularly after cases of police brutality against Black Americans. In 2020, protests erupted across the country after the killing of George Floyd by a police officer in Minneapolis, Minnesota.3. France: The "Yellow Vest" protests, which began in 2018, were marked by violence and clashes with police. Protesters demanded economic reform and an improvement in their standard of living.4. Venezuela: Protests against the government of President Nicolas Maduro in 2019 were met with violent repression from government forces. Reports emerged of police and military forces firing live ammunition at protesters.5. Turkey: In 2013, protests erupted in Istanbul over a proposed development project that threatened a park in Taksim Square. The protests turned violent when police used tear gas and water cannons to disperse the crowds.

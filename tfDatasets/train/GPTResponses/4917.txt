There are several factors that can destroy a wheat crop:1. Disease: Various fungal, bacterial, and viral diseases can infect wheat plants, causing leaf damage, stunted growth, and rapid transmission of disease among the crop.2. Pests: Insects such as aphids, thrips and mites can feed on the wheat plants, causing stress and damage to the plant. Birds and rodents can also damage the crop while feeding on the kernels.3. Weather: Drought, excessive rainfall, hailstorm, extreme heat, and cold weather can all result in significant loss of yield.4. Weeds: weeds compete with wheat plants for water, soil nutrients, and sunlight, and can significantly reduce the yield.5. Human activities: pollution, mismanagement of irrigation, and improper use of pesticides and fertilizer can harm the soil, and ultimately reduce the quality of the wheat grain.

The laws contained in the written Torah are known as the 613 commandments or mitzvot. These commandments cover a wide range of subjects including:1. Worship and ritual practices (e.g. observing the Sabbath, keeping kosher)2. Family relationships and sexual ethics (e.g. laws related to marriage and divorce, laws prohibiting incest)3. Social justice and ethical behavior (e.g. laws related to charity and caring for the stranger)4. Business and commercial practices (e.g. laws prohibiting theft and fraud)5. Criminal justice (e.g. laws related to capital punishment and fair trials)The Torah is considered the most sacred text in Judaism and is the foundation for Jewish law and tradition.

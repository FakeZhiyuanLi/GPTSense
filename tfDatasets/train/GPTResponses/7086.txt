To be protected, a work must contain a degree of originality as well as meet the criteria for copyright protection, which includes being fixed in a tangible medium of expression, having some degree of creativity or authorship, and being within one of the categories of works protected by copyright law. Additionally, the work must not have been published before, or if it has been published, the author must have obtained the necessary permissions or licenses for use. In some cases, the work must also contain a notice of copyright, and the author or owner must register the work with the appropriate government agency.

During Hellenistic times, astrology experienced a significant expansion and became more prominent in society. Astrologers were highly valued in the court of rulers, where they provided guidance on matters such as politics, military strategy, and personal affairs. The Hellenistic period saw the development of elaborate astrological systems, including the use of horoscopes, astrological charts, and the twelve zodiac signs. Greek thinkers, such as Aristarchus and Hipparchus, made important contributions to the field by developing theories on the movement of celestial bodies and the significance of planetary alignments. The popularity of astrology continued to grow during the Roman Empire and influenced the development of other esoteric practices, including alchemy and magic. Overall, astrology during the Hellenistic period was a respected and widely accepted field of study that had a significant impact on ancient Greek and Roman culture.

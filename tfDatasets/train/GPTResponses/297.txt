There are several types of institutions that are not subject to the same regulations as commercial banks. Some of these include:1. Credit unions: These are not-for-profit financial cooperatives owned by their members. They are regulated by the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA), which is a federal agency, but they have different regulations than commercial banks.2. Investment banks: These banks help companies and governments issue securities and raise capital. They are regulated by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA), not the same regulations as commercial banks.3. Hedge funds: These are investment funds that operate with a high degree of flexibility and take on more risk than traditional investment funds. They are regulated by the SEC but do not have the same regulations as commercial banks.4. Private equity firms: These firms invest in companies and provide them with capital and operational expertise to help them grow. They are regulated by the SEC but do not have the same regulations as commercial banks.5. Venture capital firms: These firms invest in early-stage companies and provide them with capital and support to help them grow. They are also regulated by the SEC but do not have the same regulations as commercial banks.

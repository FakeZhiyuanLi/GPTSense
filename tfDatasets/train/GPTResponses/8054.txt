One of the most influential figures in Islamic philosophy was Al-Farabi. He was a Persian philosopher, scientist, and musician who lived during the Islamic Golden Age. He is considered to be one of the founders of Islamic Neoplatonism and his works had a profound impact on Islamic philosophy, as well as on the philosophy of the Western world. Other influential figures in Islamic philosophy include Ibn Rushd (Averroes), Ibn Arabi, and Ibn Khaldun.

Gustav Mahler's Symphony No. 8 is also known as the Symphony of a Thousand due to the large number of performing musicians required for it. The orchestra for this symphony typically includes:- 8 vocal soloists (soprano, alto, tenor, baritone, bass, and 3 boys' voices)- Mixed choir (at least 150 singers)- Boys' choir (at least 30 singers)- Large orchestra including extra woodwinds, brass, and percussionTherefore, a performance of Mahler's Symphony No. 8 requires a very large orchestra and choir, totaling at least 300 performers, which is why it is often considered one of the most difficult pieces to perform.

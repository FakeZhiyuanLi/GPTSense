Universities typically have gardens for many reasons. Some of the most common reasons are:1. Research: Many universities use their gardens as research centers to study plant growth, soil quality, and other factors that affect agriculture and horticulture.2. Education: Gardens can be used as outdoor classrooms for students studying botany, ecology, natural resources and other related fields.3. Aesthetic Value: Universities may maintain gardens to enhance the beauty of their campuses and create a calming effect.4. Environmental Conservation: Gardens can be used for environmental conservation efforts, such as the preservation of endangered plant species or the establishment of butterfly gardens to support local pollinators.Overall, university gardens offer multiple benefits, including research opportunities, educational tools, beautification of campus and environmental conservation.

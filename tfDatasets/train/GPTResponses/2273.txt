The official documents appointing a party's representative to act on their behalf may include:1. Power of Attorney: A legal document authorizing an individual, known as the attorney-in-fact or agent, to act on behalf of another person, known as the principal.2. Letter of Authorization: A written document granting permission to a representative to act on behalf of a person or entity for a particular purpose.3. Appointment of Representative: A form used by the Social Security Administration to appoint a representative to act on behalf of a person as they apply for social security benefits.4. Proxy Statement: A document filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) that authorizes a representative to vote on behalf of shareholders at a company's annual meeting.5. Representation Agreement: A legal contract between a client and a representative that outlines the scope of the representative's duties and responsibilities.

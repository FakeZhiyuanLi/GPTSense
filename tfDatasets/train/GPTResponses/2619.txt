If there was an erroneous understanding of a fact or situation at the time of conclusion of a treaty, the following may be invalidated:1. The entire treaty may be invalidated if the erroneous understanding was a fundamental part of the treaty.2. The specific provision or clause that was affected by the erroneous understanding may be invalidated.3. The interpretation and implementation of the treaty may be affected if the erroneous understanding was not discovered until after the treaty was concluded.4. The credibility and trust between the parties involved in the treaty may be affected if the erroneous understanding was perceived as intentional deception or manipulation.5. The validity of any subsequent agreements or treaties that rely on the erroneous understanding may be affected.

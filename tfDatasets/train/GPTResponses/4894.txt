Non-primarily gold and silver coins can be valuable for several reasons, including:1. Rarity: Some coins were minted in limited quantities, making them rare and sought-after by collectors.2. Historical significance: Coins that were minted during significant historical events or periods can hold value due to their connection to history.3. Condition: The condition of a coin can significantly affect its value. Coins that are well-preserved and in excellent condition will often fetch higher prices than those with significant wear and tear.4. Numismatic value: Some coins are valuable due to their unique design, error markings, or other features that make them desirable to collectors.5. Cultural significance: Coins that are specific to certain regions or cultures can hold value due to their cultural significance and appeal to collectors interested in those cultures.

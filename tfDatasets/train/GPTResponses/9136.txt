After World War II, John von Neumann worked as a consultant to various government agencies, including the Atomic Energy Commission, the National Bureau of Standards, and the Navy. He also played a key role in the development of stored-program digital computers, which paved the way for modern computing. Von Neumann is also known for his work in game theory, which has applications in economics and political science. He was a prolific author and made numerous contributions to mathematics, physics, and computer science.

During the Yugoslav Wars (1991-2001), aid was distributed to Yugoslavia by several United Nations agencies, as well as by NGOs and individual countries. Some of the major agencies that provided aid were:1. United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)2. International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC)3. United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)4. United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF)5. World Health Organization (WHO)6. European Union Humanitarian Aid Office (ECHO)The United States also provided aid to Yugoslavia during this period through the United States Agency for International Development (USAID) and other channels.

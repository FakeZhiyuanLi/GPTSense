Hyponatremia refers to a condition where the level of sodium in the blood is lower than normal or below 135 mEq/L. It can occur due to various reasons such as drinking too much water, kidney problems, hormonal imbalances, certain medications, and other medical conditions. Hyponatremia can cause symptoms such as headache, nausea, vomiting, confusion, seizures, and even coma or death in severe cases.

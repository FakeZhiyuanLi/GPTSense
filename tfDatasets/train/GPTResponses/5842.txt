Melbourne is located in the southern hemisphere and experiences a wide variety of weather patterns throughout the year. However, typically, the type of weather fronts that are responsible for severe weather such as gales, thunderstorms, hail, and heavy rain in Melbourne are cold fronts. When a cold front approaches, it brings colder air and can cause a sudden drop in temperature, which can create thunderstorm activity and potentially severe weather. These fronts often have strong winds associated with them, which can bring damaging gusts that cause widespread power outages and structural damage. Additionally, these fronts can lead to heavy rainfall or even hail, which can also cause flooding and damage.

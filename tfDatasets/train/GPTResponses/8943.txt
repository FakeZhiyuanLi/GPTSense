In Czech language, primary word stress is usually on the first syllable, but there are a few exceptions where the stress falls on the second or even the third syllable:1. Words with long vowels or diphthongs: Examples: Okénko (window), Věž (tower), Léto (summer), Sójový (soybean).2. Compound words: Examples: bezpečnostní kamera (security camera), rodičovská dovolená (parental leave), americký fotbal (American football).3. Words with prefixes: Examples: protihrad (counter-hail), neprůhledný (non-transparent), představitel (representative).4. Loanwords: Examples: telefon (telephone), komunikace (communication), identita (identity).5. Certain names and surnames: Examples: Václav (Wenceslas), František (Francis), Jan (John), Karel (Charles).

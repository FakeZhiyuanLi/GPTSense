Before the fall of Saddam Hussein's regime in 2003, the Iraqi army possessed a large arsenal of modern and Soviet-era weapons, including:1. Main battle tanks: T-72, T-55, T-62, Type 69, and Type 59.2. Armored vehicles: BMP-1, BMP-2, BTR-50, BTR-60, and MT-LB.3. Artillery: 122mm D-30 howitzers, M-46 130mm field artillery, M-1938 122mm field guns, and 105mm M101A1 howitzers.4. Anti-aircraft weapons: SA-2, SA-3, SA-6, ZPU-4, ZU-23-2, and S-60 57mm AA guns.5. Small arms: AK-47, AKM, PK machine guns, RPK light machine guns, RPG-7s, and SVD rifles.6. Air force: Mig-21, Mig-23, Su-22, and Mirage F1 fighter planes, and various helicopters.7. Navy: Several small gunboats and patrol craft.However, during the US-led invasion of Iraq in 2003 and subsequent insurgency, much of the Iraqi army's equipment was destroyed or captured by coalition forces. The modernization of the Iraqi army since then has been largely dependent on foreign assistance, and it currently possesses a variety of weapons systems acquired from the US, Russia, and other countries.

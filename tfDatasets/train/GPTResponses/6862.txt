There are several acts and laws that cover tort damages from police brutality cases, including:1. Civil Rights Act of 1871 (Section 1983): This federal law provides individuals with the right to sue government officials, including police officers, for violating their constitutional rights.2. Fourth Amendment: This amendment protects individuals from unreasonable searches and seizures, and excessive use of force by law enforcement officers.3. Fourteenth Amendment: This amendment guarantees equal protection under the law and due process of law for all individuals.4. State tort laws: Each state has its own set of laws that offer remedies to individuals who have suffered harm due to police misconduct.5. Qualified immunity: This legal doctrine protects government officials, including police officers, from being held personally liable for actions performed in their official capacity.It's worth noting that navigating the legal system can be complex, especially when dealing with police misconduct cases. It's important to seek the advice of a qualified attorney who specializes in civil rights and police misconduct cases.

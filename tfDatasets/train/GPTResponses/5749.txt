Many writers, activists, and scholars have focused on the issue of racial and cultural confrontation. Some prominent examples include W.E.B. Du Bois, James Baldwin, Angela Davis, bell hooks, Frantz Fanon, Audre Lorde, and Ta-Nehisi Coates. These individuals have explored the many ways in which racism, colonialism, and cultural imperialism operate to maintain power structures that privilege some groups while marginalizing and oppressing others. They have also discussed strategies for challenging these structures and promoting equity and justice for all.

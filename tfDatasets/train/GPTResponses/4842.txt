The Federal Reserve issues several types of notes:1. Federal Reserve Notes: These are the most common type of U.S. currency in circulation. They are issued in denominations of $1, $2, $5, $10, $20, $50, and $100.2. Silver Certificates: These were originally issued from 1878 to 1964 and could be exchanged for silver dollars. They are no longer in circulation and are considered collectible items.3. Gold Certificates: These were issued from 1863 to 1933 and could be exchanged for gold coins. They are also no longer in circulation and are considered collectible items.4. National Bank Notes: These were issued by individual banks from 1863 to 1935 and were backed by U.S. government bonds. They are also no longer in circulation and are considered collectible items.

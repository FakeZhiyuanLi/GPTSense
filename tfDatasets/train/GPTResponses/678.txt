During the Scramble for Africa, several new Portuguese coastal towns were developed in present-day Angola and Mozambique, including:1. Luanda - the capital of Angola, developed in the late 16th century as a port for the transatlantic slave trade.2. Benguela - developed in the 17th century as a trading post for the ivory and slave trade.3. Lobito - developed in the late 19th century as a port for the export of copper and other minerals from the Katanga region.4. Maputo - the capital of Mozambique, developed in the late 19th century as a port for the export of gold, ivory, and slaves.5. Beira - developed in the late 19th century as a port for the export of gold and other minerals from the inland regions of Mozambique.

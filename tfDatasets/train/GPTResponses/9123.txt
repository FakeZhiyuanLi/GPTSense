If you are 65 years old and have obstructive airway disease, you are more likely to have other conditions such as:1. Cardiovascular diseases: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) increases the risk of developing cardiovascular diseases, such as heart attack, stroke, and heart failure.2. Osteoporosis: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) increases the risk of developing osteoporosis, a condition that weakens bones and increases the risk of fractures.3. Respiratory infections: People with obstructive airways disease are more likely to develop respiratory infections such as pneumonia, influenza, and bronchitis.4. Anxiety and depression: COPD can cause anxiety and depression due to the limitations it imposes on daily activities and the progression of the disease.5. Sleep apnea: Obstructive airway disease can cause sleep apnea, a condition where the breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep.6. Lung cancer: Long-term smokers with COPD are at higher risk for developing lung cancer.7. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD): COPD can increase the risk of developing GERD, a condition that causes acid reflux and heartburn.

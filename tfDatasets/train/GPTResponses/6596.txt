The 24 Hours of Daytona is an endurance sports car race held annually at the Daytona International Speedway in Daytona Beach, Florida. It is a 24-hour race that takes place over the course of a single weekend in late January or early February. The race draws some of the top sports car drivers from around the world, and is considered one of the most prestigious endurance races in the world. The event was first held in 1962 and has been a part of the International Motor Sports Association (IMSA) since its founding in 1969.

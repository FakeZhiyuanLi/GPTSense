One of the most recognizable parts of Eritrean culture that can be offered when visiting friends as well as being a daily staple of life is injera. Injera is a type of sourdough flatbread made from teff flour and is typically used as a serving utensil for stews and other dishes. It is also often eaten on its own with various dips and spreads. Injera is an essential part of Eritrean cuisine and plays a significant role in the country's customs and traditions.

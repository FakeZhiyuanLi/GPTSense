During the Baroque era, there was less standardization in several aspects of music such as:1) Tuning systems: There were various tuning systems used by different musicians and regions, resulting in differences in tonality and pitch.2) Ornamentation: The use of embellishments and ornaments varied between composers and performers, creating a unique style and interpretation.3) Notation: Music notation was less standardized, and composers used different symbols and techniques to indicate dynamics, articulation, and expression.4) Performance practice: Interpretation and performance style varied depending on the musician, ensemble, and location, resulting in differences in tempo, phrasing, and articulation.5) Instrumentation: The variety of instruments used during the Baroque era was vast, and different ensembles employed different instruments resulting in differences in timbre and texture.

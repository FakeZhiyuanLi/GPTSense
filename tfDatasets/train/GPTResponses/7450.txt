The European Union has engaged in various activities in response to the reforms made in Burma, also known as Myanmar.1. Lifting of sanctions: The EU has lifted most of the economic sanctions that were imposed on Myanmar during its military regime.2. Development aid: The EU has increased its development aid to Myanmar to support its economic and political reforms.3. Trade relations: The EU has granted Myanmar access to the Everything But Arms (EBA) scheme, which allows duty-free and quota-free access to the EU market for all products except arms and ammunition.4. Dialogue and cooperation: The EU has engaged in dialogue and cooperation with Myanmar on various issues, such as human rights, democracy, and peace-building.5. Election observation: The EU has also sent election observation missions to Myanmar to ensure the transparency and fairness of the electoral process.Overall, the EU's engagement with Myanmar is aimed at supporting its path towards democracy, peace, and development.

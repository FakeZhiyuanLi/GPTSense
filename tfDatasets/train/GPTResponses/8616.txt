Russia saw itself as the protector of Slavic peoples in the region and felt a duty to defend Serbia, a fellow Slavic nation, against any aggression. Therefore, Russia wanted Austria-Hungary to refrain from any actions that could be interpreted as an attack on Serbia, and to allow Russia to intervene on Serbia's behalf without interference. This demand ultimately led to the outbreak of World War I when Germany, Austria-Hungary's ally, declared war on Russia in support of their ally.

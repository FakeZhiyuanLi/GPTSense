Halite is a mineral form of sodium chloride, commonly known as table salt. It is a crystalline mineral that occurs in sedimentary and evaporite deposits. Halite is usually colorless, white, or gray, but it can also be pink, yellow, or red due to impurities. It has a hardness of 2.5 on the Mohs scale and a cubic crystal structure. Halite is a valuable source of salt for food and industrial applications, including chemical production and water treatment.

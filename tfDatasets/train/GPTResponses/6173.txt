Ubaidullah bin Ziyad was an Umayyad governor who ruled over Kufa in Iraq in the 7th century. He played a crucial role in the Islamic history by suppressing the uprising led by Imam Hussein, the grandson of Prophet Muhammad, in the Battle of Karbala. After the battle, he continued to rule over Kufa, Basra, and other regions until his death. His rule was marked by cruelty and oppression, and he was known for his violent tactics in suppressing dissent and rebellion.

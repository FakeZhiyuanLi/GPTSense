The Enlightenment thinkers championed the primacy of reason, individual liberty, tolerance, and the pursuit of knowledge. They believed that human beings are capable of using their own reasoning to question everything, including religious and political authority. They sought to usher in a new era of freedom, equality, and progress by using science and empirical observation to understand the natural world and human society. Ultimately, the Enlightenment was an attack on the old order and a movement towards a more rational, open, and progressive society.

A material breach of treaty obligations may result in various consequences, such as:1. Termination of the treaty: The non-breaching party may choose to terminate the treaty and its obligations under it.2. Invocation of dispute resolution mechanisms: The non-breaching party may use the dispute resolution mechanisms provided in the treaty to seek a resolution for the breach.3. Legal action: The non-breaching party may take legal action against the violating party in domestic or international courts or tribunals.4. Loss of credibility: The violating party may lose credibility in the international community and suffer reputational damage.5. Retaliation: The non-breaching party may retaliate by taking certain measures such as economic sanctions, trade restrictions or diplomatic measures against the violating party.6. Damage to bilateral or multilateral relationships: The breach may harm the peaceful and cooperative relationships that were fostered by the treaty.

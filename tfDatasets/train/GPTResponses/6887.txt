Monasteries had gardens for several reasons:1. Food production: Monasteries were often self-sufficient communities, and they needed to grow their own food. Gardens provided a source of fresh vegetables, fruits, and herbs throughout the year.2. Medicinal purposes: Many monks had knowledge of herbal medicine, and they used plants grown in the monastery gardens to treat various ailments.3. Spiritual practice: Gardens were often seen as a way to connect with God and nature, and many monasteries had special cloister gardens where monks could meditate and pray.4. Education and research: Monks were often involved in scientific and agricultural research, and gardens were used to study and experiment with different plant varieties and growing techniques.Overall, monastery gardens served a practical purpose in supplying food and medicine, as well as a spiritual purpose in providing a connection with God and nature.

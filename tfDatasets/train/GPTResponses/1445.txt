Autoimmune disorders: Clinical immunology studies diseases where the body's immune system attacks its own cells, tissues or organs, causing conditions such as lupus, rheumatoid arthritis or multiple sclerosis.2. Immunodeficiency disorders: Clinical immunology also studies diseases or conditions where the immune system fails to protect the body from infections and diseases, such as HIV/AIDS, primary immunodeficiency disorders, and secondary immunodeficiency disorders, that can occur as a result of chemotherapy or other treatments for other diseases.3. Allergic reactions: Clinical immunology studies the immune system's response to allergens, such as food, pollen or dust, which can lead to allergic reactions, such as asthma or hives.

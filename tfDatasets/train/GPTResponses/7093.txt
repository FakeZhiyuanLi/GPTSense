Pesticides can protect plants from a variety of threats, including:1. Insect infestations: Pesticides can be used to control and prevent damage from insects such as aphids, beetles, and caterpillars.2. Fungal and bacterial diseases: Pesticides can prevent the growth and spread of fungal and bacterial diseases such as powdery mildew, blight, and rust.3. Weed growth: Herbicides are a type of pesticide that can be used to control the growth of weeds which can compete with crops for nutrients, water, and light.4. Rodent and animal damage: Pesticides can protect crops from damage caused by rodents and animals such as deer, rabbits, and birds.5. Environmental stresses: Pesticides can help plants resist environmental stresses such as drought, extreme temperatures, and salinity.

The United States Statutes at Large is a collection of laws passed by Congress and signed by the President that have been published in chronological order since the first session of the U.S. Congress in 1789. The Statutes at Large is an official record of the laws passed by Congress and is used as a primary source for researching federal legislation. It contains the text of all public laws, private laws, and concurrent resolutions passed by Congress in a given year. The Statutes at Large is published annually by the Office of the Federal Register, National Archives and Records Administration.

The Rule of St. Augustine is a set of guidelines and insights meant to guide communities of Christian clergy and monastics. The Rule, written by St. Augustine of Hippo in the 4th century, emphasized the importance of maintaining a communal and spiritual way of life, in which individuals lived in service to God and to one another. The Rule also embraced the concept of individual spiritual development, and emphasized the importance of prayer, study, and meditation in achieving this goal. Overall, the Rule of St. Augustine represented a vision for a Christian communal life, rooted in the teachings of Jesus Christ and dedicated to serving God and the wider community.

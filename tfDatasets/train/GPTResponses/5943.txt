There are several types of integration that large corporations often engage in, including:1. Vertical integration: This is when a company integrates with suppliers, distributors or retailers in its supply chain in order to gain more control over the production process and reduce costs.2. Horizontal integration: This is when a company merges with or acquires another company in the same industry in order to increase market share and gain a competitive edge.3. Conglomerate integration: This is when a company merges with or acquires businesses in unrelated industries in order to diversify its product offerings and reduce risk.4. Strategic alliances: This is when two or more companies come together to form a partnership in order to achieve common goals such as sharing resources or entering new markets.5. Joint ventures: This is when two or more companies create a new entity in order to share resources, knowledge and risk in a specific market or product.

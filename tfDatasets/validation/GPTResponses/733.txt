The cylinders are typically made of steel or aluminum and are filled with hydraulic fluid, which is pressurized by a piston to lift and lower the elevator cab. When electrolytic corrosion occurs in the cylinder, it can lead to pitting, cracking, or other structural damage, ultimately leading to cylinder failure and potentially catastrophic elevator failure. Regular maintenance and inspections can help prevent such malfunctions and ensure the safe operation of hydraulic elevators.

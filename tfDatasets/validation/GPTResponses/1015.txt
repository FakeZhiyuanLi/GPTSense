The purpose of the 1889 Universal Exposition, also known as the Exposition Universelle, was to showcase advancements in science, technology, and culture from around the world. It was specifically held to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution and to showcase France as a leading industrial and cultural power. The most famous attraction at the exposition was the Eiffel Tower, which was built as a temporary structure to serve as the entrance arch to the exposition. The exposition drew millions of visitors from around the world and established Paris as a center of modernity, progress, and innovation.

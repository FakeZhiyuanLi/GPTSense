The Great Appalachian Valley is a geographic region that extends from Canada to Alabama and forms the easternmost range of the Appalachian Mountains. It provides a natural corridor for transportation and commerce, as well as being an important agricultural and industrial region. The valley also supports a diverse ecosystem of wildlife and plant species, including forests, wetlands, and grasslands. Additionally, the valley is an important source of water for millions of people and is home to many historic sites, cultural landmarks, and recreational areas.

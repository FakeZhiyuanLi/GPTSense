The important esoteric interpreter of the Quran in the 11th century was Abu Hamid Muhammad ibn Muhammad al-Ghazali, also known as Al-Ghazali. He was a prominent Islamic theologian, jurist, philosopher, and mystic who wrote extensively on Islamic spirituality and mysticism, including his famous work, "The Revival of the Religious Sciences". Al-Ghazali's interpretations of the Quran have greatly influenced Islamic philosophy and spirituality to this day.

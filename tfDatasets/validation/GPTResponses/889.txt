There are many examples of violations of civil rights in the United States, including:1) Racial discrimination, such as racial profiling or unequal treatment based on race.2) Denial of equal protection and due process under the law, including denial of voting rights and access to education.3) Police brutality and excessive use of force, particularly against marginalized communities.4) Religious discrimination or restrictions on religious freedom.5) Discrimination based on gender or sexual orientation, such as denying marriage rights or employment opportunities.6) Violation of privacy rights, such as through warrantless surveillance or data collection.7) Infringement on freedom of speech or expression, such as censorship or restrictions on peaceful protest.

Archaeological artifacts are physical objects that have been created or modified by humans. They can include tools, weapons, pottery, clothing, jewelry, buildings, and art.Faunal remains are the bones, teeth, and other evidence of animal life. They can provide insights into the diet, hunting practices, and environmental conditions of past societies.Human altered landscapes are areas of the environment that have been modified by human activity. These can include things like terraced fields, irrigation systems, roads, and buildings. They can provide clues to the social, economic, and technological systems of past societies.All three of these types of evidence are important for understanding past human societies and their interactions with the environment. They allow archaeologists to reconstruct past lifeways, economies, and cultural practices.

Hobbes was a British philosopher who is often considered as one of the most influential political thinkers of the modern era. He was a materialist philosopher who believed that all phenomena in the world was derived from matter and motion. He was also a social contract theorist, who argued that individuals give up certain rights to a sovereign, in return for protection and security. Therefore, Hobbes can be classified as a political philosopher, a materialist philosopher, and a social contract theorist.

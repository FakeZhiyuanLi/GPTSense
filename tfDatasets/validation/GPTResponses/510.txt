Styles in France have evolved over time, but some styles that were initially present include:1. Gothic style - This style originated in the late 12th century and lasted until the 16th century. It is characterized by pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses.2. Renaissance style - This style emerged in the 16th century and was inspired by the classical art and architecture of ancient Greece and Rome. It is characterized by symmetry, proportion, and the use of classical orders such as Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian.3. Baroque style - This style emerged in the 17th century and was characterized by elaborate ornamentation, dramatic gestures, and grandeur. It was often used in the decoration of churches, palaces, and public buildings.4. Rococo style - This style emerged in the early 18th century and was a more playful and ornate version of the Baroque style. It featured delicate ornamentation, curving lines, and pastel colors.5. Neoclassical style - This style emerged in the late 18th century and was a revival of the classical Greek and Roman styles. It emphasized simplicity, clarity, and reason, and was often used in the decoration of public buildings such as museums, libraries, and courthouses.

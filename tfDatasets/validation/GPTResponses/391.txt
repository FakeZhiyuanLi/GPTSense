Emperor Ming (also known as Emperor Ming of Han) of the Han Dynasty created several offices, including:1. Grand Commandant: The highest military and civil office in the central government.2. Censorate: A supervisory agency that monitored the conduct of officials and punished wrongdoers.3. Imperial Secretariat: A central administration office responsible for drafting edicts, official communications, and handling the emperor's correspondence.4. Bureau of Agriculture: An agency responsible for managing agriculture policies, canal system, and water management.5. Ministry of Rites: An agency responsible for ceremonial rituals, foreign relations, and managing the education and selection of officials.6. Ministry of Justice: An agency that managed the judicial system, including courts and prisons.7. Commission for the Standardization of Measures: An agency that standardized measurements and weights used throughout the empire.

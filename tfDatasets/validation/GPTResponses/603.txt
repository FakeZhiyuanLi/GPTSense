The Glasgow Tower is a free-standing tower located in Glasgow, Scotland, and is the tallest tower in Scotland. It was opened in 2001 and is 127 meters tall. The tower is a unique structure that is capable of rotating 360 degrees and providing panoramic views of the city of Glasgow. It is a popular tourist attraction, offering visitors an experience unlike any other in the city. The tower was designed by architect Richard Horden and is part of the Glasgow Science Centre complex, which includes a planetarium, an IMAX cinema, and interactive science exhibitions.

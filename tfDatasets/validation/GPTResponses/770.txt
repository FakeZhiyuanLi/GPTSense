Educational psychology can be understood as a field of study that explores the cognitive, emotional, social, and behavioral aspects of learning and how they relate to educational settings. It involves studying learning processes in individuals and groups, evaluating teaching methods, and developing strategies to improve the effectiveness of learning and teaching. This field draws upon research and theories from various disciplines, including psychology, neuroscience, sociology, anthropology, and education. Educational psychology also focuses on understanding how teachers and students interact in the classroom and how to promote positive relationships between them to enhance learning outcomes. It also involves the development and implementation of assessment tools to evaluate learning outcomes and measure the effectiveness of educational programs. Overall, educational psychology aims to provide insights into how people learn and how to facilitate effective learning and teaching in diverse educational contexts.

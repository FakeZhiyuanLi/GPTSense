1. College: This is the most commonly recognized form of postsecondary or higher education. It typically involves higher learning institutions such as universities, colleges or technical schools where students spend four or more years earning degrees, diplomas, or certificates in a specific area of study.2. Vocational or Trade schools: These are schools that offer training programs for specific jobs, trades or industries such as nursing, automotive repair, electrician, welding, and culinary arts. They offer shorter-term courses to help students gain the necessary skills to enter the workforce quickly.

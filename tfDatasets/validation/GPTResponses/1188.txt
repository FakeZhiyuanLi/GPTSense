1. Economic: In later times, great powers were also described in terms of their economic prowess. Countries with strong and diverse economies that were able to generate wealth and wield significant financial clout in the world were seen as great powers.2. Cultural: Another capacity in which great powers were described was their cultural impact and influence. Countries with a rich history, artistic and intellectual achievements, and a broad global reach through diplomacy, trade, and other means were considered great powers in this sense. They were often seen as setting trends and standards in the areas of fashion, politics, music, literature, and other cultural domains.

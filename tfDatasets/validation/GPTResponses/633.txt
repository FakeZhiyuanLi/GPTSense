The Greeks wanted to impart their cultural traditions, values, and knowledge to their descendants. They believed in the importance of education, curiosity, and critical thinking. They also valued courage, loyalty, and honor, as well as the pursuit of beauty and harmony. The Greeks wanted their descendants to appreciate and preserve their language, literature, art, philosophy, and religion. Additionally, they believed in the importance of democracy and civic responsibility, and wanted their descendants to be active and engaged citizens of their communities.

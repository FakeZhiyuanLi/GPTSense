Primary school typically consists of the first six years of compulsory education for children aged between 5 and 11 years old. It is also known as elementary or grade school in some countries.The curriculum typically includes the core subjects of English, mathematics, science, and social studies. Other subjects may include art, music, physical education, and foreign languages.Primary school also focuses on developing key skills such as critical thinking, problem-solving, communication, and collaboration. Students learn through a range of activities including classroom-based lessons, group work, practical activities, and field trips.Assessment is also an important part of primary school, with regular tests and exams to evaluate student progress. Teachers may also provide feedback through written reports or parent-teacher conferences.Overall, primary school aims to provide children with a solid foundation of knowledge and skills to prepare them for further education and lifelong learning.

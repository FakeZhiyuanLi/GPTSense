There were several cultures that were well established by the 6th century BC, including:1. Greek culture, which had flourished in the city-states of Athens, Sparta, and Corinth, among others.2. Persian culture, which had emerged as a powerful force in the Middle East, with a sophisticated system of government, art, and literature.3. Chinese culture, which had developed the philosophy of Confucianism and established the Zhou dynasty.4. Indian culture, which had given rise to Buddhism and Hinduism, as well as the Maurya dynasty.5. Egyptian culture, which had survived the decline of the New Kingdom and was still a major force in the Mediterranean world.6. Babylonian culture, which had developed an advanced system of mathematics and astronomy, as well as a powerful empire under King Nebuchadnezzar.

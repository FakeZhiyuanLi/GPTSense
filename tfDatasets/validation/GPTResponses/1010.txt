Courbet, Manet, Monet, and Renoir were all prominent French artists who were associated with the Impressionist movement. They created artworks that were characterized by their innovative use of light, color, and brushwork to capture fleeting moments in time. Courbet was known for his monumental Realist paintings, Manet for his modernist approach to painting, Monet for his landscape paintings and series of studies of light and color, and Renoir for his depictions of leisure and everyday life.

The Battle of Gallipoli and the Siege of Kut resulted in significant losses for the Allied forces and a major setback for their campaigns in World War I. In the Battle of Gallipoli, the Allied forces suffered around 250,000 casualties, while the Ottoman Empire lost about 220,000 soldiers. The Siege of Kut ended in the surrender of the British forces, resulting in the loss of around 10,000 soldiers as prisoners of war. These events led to a significant blow to the morale of the Allied forces, and they had to rethink their strategies for achieving victory in the war.

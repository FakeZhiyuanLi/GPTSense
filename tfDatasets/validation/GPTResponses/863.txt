The Silk Road facilitated the transactions of customs and ways of life between the people of the Asian continent and the Greeks. This ancient network of trade routes connected China, India, Persia, and other regions of Asia with the Mediterranean world, allowing for the exchange of goods, ideas, and cultures. The Greeks were active traders along the Silk Road and were exposed to the arts, sciences, and religions of the East. The Silk Road played a significant role in shaping the history of both the Asian continent and the Greeks.

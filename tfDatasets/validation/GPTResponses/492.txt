The book that brought appeal for classic interior to masses is "Decorating is Fun!: How to Be Your Own Decorator" by Dorothy Draper. The book was published in 1939 and became a best-seller due to its accessible and easy-to-follow advice on interior design. Draper's style was characterized by bold and colorful prints, mixing old and new pieces, and creating a sense of drama and glamour in the home. Her book promoted the idea that anyone could create a beautiful home without needing a large budget or professional assistance, making classic interior design accessible to a wider audience.

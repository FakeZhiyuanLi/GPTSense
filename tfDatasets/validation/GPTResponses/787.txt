Akira Kurosawa (1910-1998) was a Japanese film director and screenwriter, widely regarded as one of the most influential filmmakers in the history of cinema. He directed over 30 films, including classics such as "Seven Samurai," "Rashomon," "Yojimbo," and "Throne of Blood." His films often explored themes of honor, loyalty, and the conflict between tradition and modernity, and he was known for his innovative techniques and use of camera angles, lighting, and sound. Kurosawa's work has had a significant impact on filmmakers worldwide, and his films continue to be celebrated for their visual and narrative power.

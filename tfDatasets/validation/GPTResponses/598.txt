The Greeks had great reverence for their forebears and considered them to be divine beings who played a significant role in shaping their culture and history. They believed that their forebears had created the world and that their gods and goddesses had descended from them. Greeks also believed in the existence of Heroes - legendary figures who had supernatural powers and were responsible for founding their cities, establishing their laws, and defending their people against enemies. These ancestral figures were often the subject of epic poems and dramas, and the Greeks celebrated them through festivals and sacrifices. Overall, the Greeks honored their forebears by preserving their memory through storytelling and mythology, which helped to reinforce their identity as a people.

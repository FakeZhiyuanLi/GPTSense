The Greeks offered the Ottomans access to their extensive shipping and trading networks, which allowed the Ottoman Empire to expand their trade routes and increase their economic power. The Greeks were also skilled in various crafts and industries, translating and preserving ancient Greek philosophy and science, which greatly benefitted the Ottoman Empire's cultural and intellectual development. Additionally, the Greeks were essential in helping the Ottomans maintain control over the Mediterranean and Aegean Seas, which were crucial for the empire's military and economic interests.

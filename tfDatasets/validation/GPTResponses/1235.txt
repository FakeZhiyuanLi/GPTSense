Jules Verne conducted his research for his stories through various means such as reading books, scientific journals, newspapers, and magazines. He also gathered information from experts in different fields, including scientists, explorers, and engineers. Verne traveled extensively to research the settings and environments of his stories, including visiting the Sahara Desert and the North Pole. Additionally, he was known to consult with his brother, who was a naval officer and provided him with technical knowledge and expertise.

The Sainte-Chapelle in Paris, France, is a 13th-century building that is an example of stained glass walls. It was built in the Gothic style and has 15 towering stained glass windows that depict biblical scenes. The windows are arranged in three tiers, with the lower windows depicting scenes from the Old Testament, the middle windows depicting scenes from the life of Christ, and the upper windows depicting scenes from the Book of Revelation. The Saint-Chapelle is considered one of the most beautiful and impressive examples of Gothic architecture in the world.

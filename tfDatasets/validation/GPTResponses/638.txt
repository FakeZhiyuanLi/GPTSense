During the Common Era of Greece, the label of Hellenes came to stand for the Greek-speaking people who shared a common culture, language, and tradition rooted in ancient Greece. It was a term used to distinguish the Greeks from other cultures and civilizations. The term also represented a sense of pride in Greek civilization and its contributions to the world, including philosophy, literature, mathematics, and art. The Hellenes were seen as the heirs of the ancient Greek civilization and its legacy. The label of Hellenes played an essential role in shaping the identity of Greeks throughout the Common Era.

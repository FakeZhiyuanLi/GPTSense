Ashikaga Yoshimasa (1436-1490) was the eighth shogun of the Ashikaga shogunate in feudal Japan. He ruled from 1449 to 1473 and was known for his patronage of the arts and culture. Under his reign, the Higashiyama culture flourished, and he commissioned many famous works of art, including the Silver Pavilion in Kyoto. Yoshimasa is also known for his involvement in the Onin War, a civil war that lasted over a decade and devastated Kyoto. He abdicated his position as shogun in 1473, passing the title to his brother, Ashikaga Yoshimi.

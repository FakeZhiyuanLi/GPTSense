The key elements of neoclassical architecture include a focus on classical forms and motifs, such as columns and pediments a symmetrical design with a strong sense of balance and proportion a preference for clean lines and simple geometries and an emphasis on harmony and order. Additionally, neoclassical buildings often incorporate elements of the natural world, such as foliage and ornamentation inspired by plants and animals. Taken together, these features communicate a sense of elegance, sophistication, and timeless beauty to viewers.

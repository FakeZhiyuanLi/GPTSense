Europeans came into contact with other groups through exploration, colonization, and trade.Explorers such as Christopher Columbus, Vasco da Gama, and Ferdinand Magellan sailed to find new trade routes and resources. They encountered indigenous people in the Americas, Africa, and Asia.Colonization followed, with European powers establishing settlements and political control over territories in the Americas, Africa, and Asia. This brought Europeans into contact with different cultures and societies.Trade also played a role in European contact with other groups. European powers traded with other regions for spices, gold, and other valuable goods. This led to cultural exchange and interaction with people from different regions.Overall, European contact with other groups was driven by exploration, colonization, and trade, resulting in cultural exchange, conflict, and the spread of European influence across the globe.
